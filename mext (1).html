Scholarship Overview

The Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship is one of the world's most prestigious and comprehensive scholarship programs, offering approximately 9,000 scholarships annually to international students. This fully-funded program supports talented Myanmar students pursuing undergraduate, Master's, and PhD studies at top Japanese universities.

MEXT scholarships provide complete financial support including full tuition waiver, generous monthly living stipends (¥117,000-¥145,000), round-trip airfare, and comprehensive Japanese language training. For Myanmar students, applications are processed through the Myanmar Association of Japan Alumni (MAJA) in coordination with the Embassy of Japan in Yangon.

✨

What Makes MEXT Special?

MEXT is funded directly by Japan's Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science & Technology. It offers the longest scholarship duration (up to 7 years for graduate students), includes mandatory Japanese language training, and provides guaranteed university placement. Scholars become part of a prestigious global alumni network spanning over 60 years.

Application Process for Myanmar Students

STEP 1

Prepare Your Documents

Gather all required documents including transcripts, recommendation letters, research plan, and certificates. Ensure all documents are properly certified and translated.

🗓️ Start 2-3 months before deadline
STEP 2

Online Registration

Register through the Google Form provided by MAJA (Myanmar Association of Japan Alumni). You'll receive a confirmation email with further instructions.

🗓️ April 14 - May 6, 2026
STEP 3

Submit Application Package

Submit your complete application package to MAJA office in Thingangyun Township, Yangon. Can be submitted in person or by post. Keep copies of everything!

🗓️ May 7-14 (Research) / May 20-26 (UG), 2026
STEP 4

Written Examination

Attend written test at designated exam station in Yangon. Tests cover Japanese/English language proficiency and subject-specific topics related to your field.

🗓️ May 17 (Research) / May 30-31 (UG), 2026
STEP 5

Interview

Qualified candidates will be invited for an interview at the Embassy of Japan in Yangon. Be prepared to discuss your study plan, motivation, and future goals in detail.

🗓️ May 31 - June 1 (Research) / June 7-8 (UG), 2026
STEP 6

University Placement

If selected, MEXT will coordinate with Japanese universities based on your preferences. You may need to request Letters of Acceptance from your preferred professors/universities.

🗓️ June - September 2026
STEP 7

Final Notification & Departure

Successful candidates receive official notification via email. Begin visa application process and prepare for departure to Japan.

🗓️ Around June 2026 → Depart April 2027

📌 Important Application Notes:

  • The entire process is FREE. Do not pay anyone for MEXT scholarship application assistance.
  • Applications can only be submitted through official channels (MAJA for Myanmar).
  • Keep copies of all submitted documents and correspondence.
  • Check your email regularly after submission for updates.
04

Tips for Successful Application

🎯

Start Early - Plan Ahead

Begin preparation at least 1 year before application. MEXT receives thousands of applications worldwide. Strong preparation is essential for standing out.

📊

Perfect Your Research Plan

This is THE most important document for graduate applicants. Be specific, realistic, and show clear understanding of your field. Vague or overly ambitious plans are rejected.

🎓

Contact Professors Early

Research potential supervisors at Japanese universities 6+ months before applying. A professor's preliminary acceptance dramatically strengthens your application.

📝

Perfect Documentation

Ensure all documents are properly certified, translated to English, and clearly organized. Missing or incorrect documents = automatic rejection.

💪

Strong Academic Record

While minimum GPA is 2.30/3.00, successful candidates typically have 2.80+ GPA. Strong grades significantly improve your chances.

🗣️

Interview Preparation

Practice explaining your study plan clearly and confidently in English. Be ready to discuss motivation, research methodology, and post-graduation plans.

🌸

Show Japan Interest

Demonstrate genuine interest in Japanese culture, language, and society. Explain why Japan specifically is important for your academic goals.

👥

Connect with Alumni

Contact MAJA and current MEXT scholars from Myanmar. They can provide invaluable insights about the application process and life in Japan.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓

Do I need to know Japanese to apply?

No! Japanese language proficiency is NOT required at application. MEXT provides 6-12 months of intensive Japanese language training before your degree program starts. This is one of the unique benefits of MEXT scholarship.

❓

Is ¥117,000-145,000 per month enough to live in Japan?

Yes! The monthly stipend is specifically calculated to cover living expenses in Japan including accommodation, food, transportation, and personal expenses. Many MEXT scholars even manage to save money. Plus, tuition is 100% free.

❓

Can I choose which university to attend?

Yes, you can list up to 3 preferred universities on your application. MEXT will coordinate placement based on your preferences, academic background, and professor availability. However, final placement is at MEXT's discretion.

❓

How competitive is MEXT for Myanmar students?

Very competitive. Typically 200-300+ Myanmar students apply each year, but only 10-15 are selected. Success rate is around 3-5%. Strong academic record, excellent research plan, and good interview performance are essential.

❓

Can I work part-time while on MEXT scholarship?

Yes, with permission from your university and immigration. You can work up to 28 hours/week during semester and full-time during breaks. However, MEXT scholars are expected to prioritize studies.

❓

What happens after I complete my degree?

You must return to Myanmar. MEXT is designed to develop future leaders who will contribute to their home countries. However, you can return to Japan later for work or further studies through other programs.
