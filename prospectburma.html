Scholarship Overview

Prospect Burma is a UK-registered charity founded in the 1990s with a singular mission: supporting young people from Myanmar's disadvantaged backgrounds to access quality higher education abroad. Since Myanmar's universities have been widely closed since the 2021 coup, making higher education nearly impossible inside the country, Prospect Burma provides critical pathways for Myanmar youth to study at universities in 19 countries worldwide - including UK, USA, Thailand, India, Malaysia, Singapore, and more. The organization has supported over 1,500 scholars over the past three decades, with 50-100 new scholarships awarded annually.

Prospect Burma offers comprehensive full scholarships covering tuition fees (through university partnerships and fee waivers), international travel, visa costs, health insurance, accommodation, monthly subsistence allowance, and educational equipment. Beyond financial support, the programme provides holistic mentoring, pastoral care, student networking opportunities, and career guidance to ensure scholars not just survive but THRIVE in their studies abroad. The programme emphasizes supporting students who demonstrate strong commitment to returning to Myanmar and using their education to benefit their communities and rebuild the nation. With 95% of scholars returning to Myanmar within 5 years of graduation (pre-coup data), Prospect Burma has created a powerful network of educated changemakers working across all sectors.

How to Apply

1

Apply to Universities First (or Simultaneously)

Prospect Burma scholarship is SEPARATE from university admission! You must apply to universities independently. Ideally have acceptance letter when applying for scholarship, but being "in process" of university applications is acceptable. Choose universities in Prospect Burma's 19 partner countries. Research programmes, deadlines, requirements. Apply to multiple universities to increase chances!

2

Gather ALL Required Documents

Collect EVERYTHING before starting application: (1) Academic transcripts and certificates, (2) English test scores (IELTS/TOEFL), (3) 2-3 recommendation letters, (4) Valid passport (current + 6 months), (5) University acceptance letter OR application evidence, (6) Financial documents showing family need, (7) CV/resume. Scan everything as clear PDFs!

3

Read Guidance Notes Thoroughly

Visit Prospect Burma student portal and read official Guidance Notes carefully! They explain exactly what's required, how to complete each section, what makes strong application. Don't skip this - saves time and prevents mistakes! Link: students.prospectburma.org/scholarships/guidance-notes/

4

Write Powerful Personal Statement

THIS IS MOST IMPORTANT! Write compelling essay explaining: (1) Your background and financial hardship, (2) Why you chose your field of study, (3) Clear career goals, (4) HOW you will use degree to benefit Myanmar specifically, (5) Your commitment to returning/serving Myanmar communities. Be specific, passionate, genuine! Show you're mission-driven, not just seeking degree. Explain Myanmar's need for your expertise!

5

Complete Online Application Portal

Visit prospectburma.my.site.com/onlineapplications/s/new-application and create account. Fill complete application form carefully - accuracy matters! Upload all documents. Write detailed, thoughtful responses to all questions. Provide complete information about financial situation, Myanmar connection, career plans. Double-check everything before submission!

Start Application →
6

Submit Before Deadline

Submit by January 31, 2026, 23:59 GMT (London time)! Don't wait until last minute - internet issues, power cuts, technical problems can happen. Submit at least 2-3 days early to be safe. After submission, you'll receive confirmation email. Save this confirmation!

7

Prepare for Interview (If Shortlisted)

If shortlisted (March-April), Prospect Burma will contact you for interview - usually virtual via Zoom for Myanmar applicants. Prepare to discuss: background, career goals, Myanmar commitment, why you deserve scholarship. Be yourself, be passionate, show genuine dedication to Myanmar! Interview assesses motivation and character.

8

If Selected: Finalize University & Prepare Travel

If awarded scholarship (May): Confirm university admission, complete enrollment, work with Prospect Burma on visa application, book flights (funded by scholarship), attend pre-departure orientation, connect with fellow scholars. Prospect Burma guides you through entire process until you arrive at university!

📌 Critical Application Tips:

  • Myanmar Commitment is Everything: Clearly articulate how you'll serve Myanmar - this is selection priority!
  • Be Specific About Impact: Don't say "I want to help Myanmar" - explain EXACTLY how your degree enables specific contributions
  • Financial Need Documentation: Be honest and detailed about family economic situation
  • Quality Over Quantity: Thoughtful, focused application better than lengthy generic one
  • Proofread Everything: Errors suggest carelessness - have someone review your application
  • Start NOW: Less than 30 days to deadline - every day counts!
04

Pro Tips for Success

🇲🇲

Myanmar Commitment Must Be Central

Prospect Burma exists to build Myanmar's future! Your application must CENTER on Myanmar service. Don't just mention it - make it your thesis! Explain specific Myanmar challenges your field addresses: "Civil engineering degree will enable me to design earthquake-resistant schools in conflict-affected Kayah State." Show deep understanding of Myanmar's needs and how YOU specifically will address them. 95% of scholars returned to Myanmar (pre-coup) - demonstrate you're part of that tradition!

📝

Personal Story Shows Resilience

Share your journey authentically! If you overcame poverty, displacement, education disruption, family hardship - tell that story! Prospect Burma values resilience and determination. Don't just state "my family is poor" - paint picture: "My mother sells vegetables at market earning $2/day supporting 5 children. Despite studying by candlelight, I ranked top in matriculation exam..." Show you're fighter who'll succeed despite obstacles!

🎓

University Choice Strategy

Choose universities strategically: (1) UK universities have most Prospect Burma fee waiver agreements - lower costs to scholarship, (2) Thailand/India/Malaysia programmes more affordable overall - scholarship goes further, (3) Top-tier universities (Oxford, Cambridge, Harvard) very competitive - also apply to solid mid-tier universities, (4) Match programme reputation to your field's needs. Don't just chase prestigious names - find BEST fit for your Myanmar goals!

💡

Recommendation Letters That Matter

Get strong reference letters from people who KNOW you well and can speak to: academic ability, character, commitment to Myanmar, leadership potential, resilience. Teachers, professors, employers, community leaders all good options. Brief recommenders on Prospect Burma's mission - ask them to emphasize your Myanmar dedication and social impact orientation. Generic academic references are weak - need letters showing YOU'RE changemaker!

🌍

Post-2021 Coup Context

If coup affected you (CDM participation, family displacement, education disruption, persecution), explain in application! Prospect Burma understands Myanmar's crisis. If you fled country, lost family members, participated in resistance, had education stopped - share this. Shows commitment to Myanmar's future despite personal cost. Many current scholars are CDM youth, displaced students, conflict survivors. Your story matters!

📚

Field Selection Aligned With Myanmar Needs

Choose field Myanmar desperately needs: Civil engineering (infrastructure rebuilding), Medicine/Public Health (healthcare access), Education (teaching next generation), Agriculture (food security), Social Work (trauma healing), Renewable Energy (sustainable development), IT/Computer Science (digital skills), Human Rights Law (justice sector). Avoid oversaturated fields unless you have unique Myanmar application angle!

👥

Connect With Alumni Network

Prospect Burma has 1,500+ alumni network! Try to connect with past scholars (Facebook groups, LinkedIn) for advice on applications, interviews, university choices. Alumni insights invaluable! They can review your application, share interview tips, recommend universities. Many alumni are now mentors for new scholars. Building these connections early helps!

⏰

Time Zone & Technical Preparation

Deadline is 23:59 GMT (London time) = 6:29 AM February 1 Myanmar time! Don't confuse time zones. Submit well before deadline to account for internet issues, power cuts common in Myanmar. Save application frequently (portal should auto-save). Have backup internet option (mobile data, internet cafe). Download confirmation email immediately after submission. Technical problems are YOUR responsibility - submit early!
