About the University

University of Nursing, Yangon (UNY) was established in 1925 as Myanmar's first dedicated nursing training institution. Originally founded to meet the growing healthcare needs during British colonial rule, UNY has evolved into the nation's premier nursing university under the Ministry of Health and Sports. It produces approximately 500 professional nurses annually through its rigorous Bachelor of Nursing Science program.

With direct affiliations to Yangon's major teaching hospitals, UNY provides unmatched clinical training opportunities. Graduates are equipped with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills essential for modern healthcare delivery, serving in hospitals, clinics, community health centers, and international organizations across Myanmar and beyond.

Historical Milestones

1925
First Nursing School established in Yangon under British colonial administration to train hospital nurses.
1948
Continued operations post-independence, becoming central to Myanmar's healthcare workforce development.
1980s
Expanded to degree-level training with introduction of comprehensive nursing diploma programs.
2000
Upgraded to University of Nursing status with authority to award Bachelor degrees.
2015
Launched BSc (Nursing) 4-year degree program aligned with international standards.
2020
Played key role in COVID-19 response training for nurses across Myanmar's healthcare system.
