Fellowship Scholarship at Ho Chi Minh City Open University

A fully-funded doctoral programme for international researchers. Full tuition waiver, a monthly living stipend of VND 10,000,000 (~USD 400), and a teaching or research assistantship — for the entire duration of your PhD.

01

Overview

The Fellowship Scholarship is HCMC Open University's flagship doctoral programme, designed to bring talented researchers into the university's teaching and research community. Recipients receive a complete tuition waiver for the full duration of their studies, alongside a monthly living stipend of VND 10,000,000 — roughly USD 400.

In exchange, Fellowship Scholars contribute as Teaching Assistants — leading eight classes per academic year — or as Research Assistants on faculty-led projects conducted in English. Doctoral programmes typically run three to four years, combining structured coursework with original research and dissertation work.

✦

Why this matters for international PhD candidates

Fully-funded doctoral opportunities in Southeast Asia are rare. This package covers tuition and living costs while building real teaching and research experience — an unusually strong foundation for an academic career. Vietnam is close to home for many regional applicants, with a tropical climate, low cost of living, and a culture that travels well.

PhD · Doctoral degree

Full scholarship + stipend, entire duration

Structure. A coursework phase in years 1–2 covers research methodology, specialised topics, and comprehensive exams. The research phase that follows produces original work, published papers, and the dissertation. Full-time commitment is required — concurrent full-time external employment is not permitted.

Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

The research environment

  • Academic hub. Multiple universities, research institutes, and academic conferences
  • Data access. Diverse urban population and active business community for fieldwork
  • Resources. University and national libraries, plus digital databases
  • Network. A growing PhD community across HCMC's universities
  • Cost of living. A VND 10M stipend covers a modest, focused student lifestyle
  • Climate. Tropical year-round — no seasonal interruption to research
  • Connectivity. Direct 1–2 hour flights to most regional capitals

Research advantages. HCMC's dynamic economy provides an excellent research context — access to companies for business studies, a diverse population for social research, and a thriving startup ecosystem for technology research.

Two-step process. First, secure admission to the PhD programme at HCMC OU. Then apply for the Fellowship Scholarship separately, with an interview before the Scholarship Council. Not all admitted PhD students receive a Fellowship — selection is competitive.

03

How to apply

1

Identify a research area and supervisor

Explore HCMC OU's faculty in your field. Read their publications, then reach out to a few prospective supervisors by email — introduce yourself, share your research interests, and ask whether they are currently accepting PhD students. Establishing this relationship early is critical.

2

Develop a strong research proposal

Write a comprehensive proposal of 10–15 pages: research questions, literature review with a clear gap, theoretical framework, methodology, expected contributions, and timeline. Make it specific, feasible, and innovative. Proposal quality is the primary factor in admission — seek feedback from your prospective supervisor.

3

Gather academic documents

Assemble Bachelor's and Master's transcripts and certificates, an academic CV highlighting research experience and publications, two to three strong recommendation letters, English test scores, and a passport copy. Provide notarised English translations for documents in other languages.

4

Submit your PhD application

Contact HCMC OU's Graduate School for application procedures. Complete the programme application, submit all documents including your proposal, and pay any application fee. Work with your prospective supervisor to strengthen your submission.

5

Apply for the Fellowship

Once PhD admission is likely or confirmed, submit your Fellowship application separately. Indicate your preference for a Teaching or Research Assistant role and explain how your background supports it.

6

Prepare for the interview

If shortlisted, prepare to present your research proposal clearly, articulate your teaching or research capability, and demonstrate English fluency. Be ready to discuss why HCMC OU, how you'll contribute as a TA or RA, and your career goals after the PhD.

7

Accept the offer and arrange your visa

On award, accept your admission and Fellowship and sign the assistantship contract. Apply for a Vietnamese student visa using your admission letter and scholarship confirmation — the university will provide a support letter. Processing typically takes one to two weeks.

8

Relocate and begin your doctorate

Travel to Ho Chi Minh City, find accommodation (USD 100–200/month for a basic apartment), register with the university, attend orientation, and begin coursework alongside your TA or RA duties.

Critical application tips

  • Build the supervisor connection first. Don't apply blind — relationships matter.
  • Invest in proposal quality. This is the single most important factor.
  • Highlight publications. Even those from a Master's thesis carry weight.
  • Surface prior teaching or research roles. Any TA, RA, or instructor experience strengthens your case.
  • Practise your interview thoroughly. The Fellowship decision hinges on it.
04

Pro tips for success

🎓

Your proposal is everything

Spend weeks developing it. Deep literature review, a clear research gap, an innovative approach, and a feasible methodology. Comparative studies grounded in Vietnam tend to be especially strong because the local data is so accessible.

💰

The stipend is liveable in HCMC

VND 10M (~USD 400) per month is modest but workable for a focused student. A typical budget: shared accommodation ~USD 150, food ~USD 150, transport ~USD 30, utilities ~USD 30, and the rest for personal expenses.

👨‍🏫

Teaching experience is career capital

Eight classes a year is meaningful experience. Universities worldwide value teaching when hiring faculty — keep a portfolio of syllabi, slides, and student feedback throughout.

🔬

Plan your publications early

Most PhD programmes require publications for graduation. Aim for a regional literature paper in year one, empirical findings in international journals across years two and three, and your dissertation in year four.

🌏

Use HCMC as a research laboratory

A diverse population, an active business community, and a vibrant startup ecosystem make HCMC unusually rich for fieldwork — especially for cross-country comparative studies across ASEAN.

🤝

Build your peer network

There's a growing community of international PhD students across Vietnam's universities. A peer network is essential for the long stretches of independent research a doctorate requires.

📚

A PhD is a marathon

Three to four years is a long horizon. Year one is the hardest — coursework, adjustment, language. Years two and three are the most productive. Year four is for writing and defence. Pace yourself; setbacks are normal.

🎯

Plan your career from day one

Whether your goal is academia, a research institution, or industry R&D, build the CV deliberately throughout your studies — publications, conference talks, grants, and a teaching record.
