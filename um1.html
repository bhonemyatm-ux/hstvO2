About the University

ဆေးတက္ကသိုလ် (၁) ရန်ကုန်သည် မြန်မာနိုင်ငံ၏ အသက်အကြီးဆုံးနှင့် အထင်ရှားဆုံးသော ဆေးပညာသင်ကြားရေးဗဟိုဌာန ဖြစ်ပါသည်။ ၁၉၀၇ ခုနှစ်တွင် ရန်ကုန်အထွေထွေဆေးရုံ၌ Government Medical School အဖြစ် စတင်တည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပြီး ၁၉၂၇ ခုနှစ်တွင် Sir Harcourt Butler မှ အဓိကအဆောက်အဦ အုတ်မြစ်ချပေးကာ ဖွဲ့စည်းတည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပါသည်။

University of Medicine 1, Yangon (UM1) was formally established in 1927, though its roots trace back to the Government Medical School founded in 1907 at Rangoon General Hospital. By 1937, the M.B.,B.S. (Rgn) degree was recognized by the UK's General Medical Council — a distinction that cemented its global standing. Today, UM1 remains the most competitive and prestigious medical institution in Myanmar, producing generations of physicians, researchers, and health leaders.

With an annual intake of only ~400 students from the country's entire science cohort, admission to UM1 is among the hardest-won achievements in Myanmar's education system. Graduates are recognized internationally through ECFMG accreditation, opening doors to postgraduate studies and licensing in the United States, United Kingdom, and beyond.

Historical Milestones

1907
Government Medical School established at Rangoon General Hospital — the founding seed of UM1.
1923
The M.B.,B.S. course was first introduced.
1927
Main building foundation laid by Sir Harcourt Butler. Classes moved to the new campus in 1929.
1937
M.B.,B.S. (Rgn) recognized by the UK's General Medical Council — establishing international credibility.
1964
Became Institute of Medicine 1 under the University Education Act.
2005
Renamed University of Medicine 1, Yangon — its current official name.
2025
Official email domain updated to @um1yangon.edu.mm as part of digital modernization.

Undergraduate Program

The gateway to becoming a doctor in Myanmar

🩺

Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery

M.B.,B.S.

Myanmar's flagship medical degree — a rigorous 7-year program (including one year of internship) that develops competent, ethical, and compassionate physicians using an Outcome-Based Integrated Curriculum (OBIC). The M.B.,B.S. from UM1 is equivalent to an M.D. internationally.

Total Duration 7 Years (incl. 1 yr internship)
Selectivity Top percentile — ~400 seats/year
Prerequisites Biology, Chemistry, English (Science stream)
Annual Intake ~400 students (most selective in Myanmar)
Anatomy Physiology Biochemistry Pathology Microbiology Pharmacology Medicine Surgery OB/GYN Paediatrics Forensic Medicine

M.B.,B.S. Course Structure

7 years of intensive medical training

Year / Phase Duration Key Subjects
Foundation Year 1 Year Burmese, English, Math, Physics, Chemistry, Botany, Zoology
Medical 1st Year 1½ Years Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry
Medical 2nd Year 1 Year Pathology, Microbiology, Pharmacology + Clinical Postings begin
Final Part I 1 Year Forensic Medicine, Preventive & Social Medicine, Systemic Pathology
Final Part II 1½ Years Child Health, Medicine, OB/GYN, Surgery + allied specialties
House Surgeon (Internship) 1 Year Rotations in teaching hospitals — full clinical immersion

Academic & Research Projects

Advancing medicine and medical education in Myanmar

Recent
Curriculum Reform

Outcome-Based Integrated Curriculum (OBIC)

UM1 successfully pioneered Myanmar's transition to the modern OBIC system — a student-centered approach that integrates basic sciences with early clinical exposure, replacing the traditional subject-siloed model. This reform aligned UM1's training with international medical education standards.

Medical Education Curriculum Innovation Student-Centered Learning

International Recognition

ECFMG Accreditation Maintenance

UM1 remains one of only 5 Myanmar medical universities recognized by the Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG), enabling graduates to sit USMLE examinations and pursue residencies in the United States and other countries.
