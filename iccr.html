Scholarship Overview

The Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR) is an autonomous organization of the Government of India that administers India's flagship international scholarship programme. ICCR awards approximately 3,000+ scholarships annually to foreign students from 180+ countries, with Myanmar receiving a dedicated allocation of 36 scholarships per academic year. These scholarships enable Myanmar students to pursue undergraduate, postgraduate (Master's), and doctoral (PhD) programmes at top Indian universities and institutes across India.

ICCR scholarships cover full tuition fees, monthly living stipend, accommodation allowance, and one-way economy airfare. Students can apply to study at prestigious institutions including IITs (Indian Institutes of Technology), NITs (National Institutes of Technology), central universities like Delhi University and Jawaharlal Nehru University, agricultural universities, and specialized institutes. The scholarship is managed through the A2A (Africa to Asia) online portal, making applications accessible and transparent. With India's growing global prominence, affordable living costs, and cultural proximity to Myanmar, ICCR represents an excellent opportunity for comprehensive higher education.

✨

Why Choose ICCR Scholarship?

India offers EXCEPTIONAL value for Myanmar students! ICCR provides FULL FUNDING (tuition + monthly stipend ₹18,000-25,000 = ~$215-300 USD + accommodation allowance ₹20,400-28,800/year). India is culturally close to Myanmar (Buddhist heritage, similar values), very affordable (₹10,000-15,000/month = ~$120-180 living costs), and geographically accessible. You can study at world-class IITs, NITs, and universities! 36 dedicated slots for Myanmar ensure good chances! Plus, apply to 5 universities simultaneously to maximize options! English-taught programmes available, and Indian degrees are globally recognized. NO tuition fees to pay!

03

How to Apply

1

Get Documents Attested (START EARLY!)

Take ALL educational certificates, degrees, and transcripts to Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Myanmar for attestation/certification. This is MANDATORY and takes 2-4 weeks! Do this BEFORE portal opens to avoid rush. For Master's/PhD applicants: also apply for AIU Equivalence Certificate early.

2

Research Indian Universities & Programmes

Visit A2A portal when it opens (Feb 20) to view list of empanelled universities. Research programmes, eligibility criteria, and specific requirements. You can apply to UP TO 5 universities/institutes in order of preference! Check IITs, NITs, central universities, and specialized institutes.

Visit A2A Portal →
3

Register on A2A Portal

Create account on A2A (Africa to Asia) scholarship portal: https://a2ascholarships.iccr.gov.in. Use valid email address and create strong password. You'll receive login credentials. Keep these safe for future reference!

4

Complete Online Application

Login to A2A portal and fill application form carefully with accurate information. Upload scanned copies of: attested certificates, transcripts, passport, photograph. Select UP TO 5 universities in preference order. Write compelling statement of purpose explaining why you want to study in India and your career goals.

5

Submit English Proficiency Evidence

Prepare 500-word essay in English (if required) demonstrating language proficiency. Some universities may ask for IELTS/TOEFL - check specific requirements. If you studied in English medium school, mention this in application.

6

Submit Application & Monitor Status

Review all information carefully before final submission. Submit application through A2A portal by April 30, 2026. After submission, regularly check portal and email for updates. Universities will review applications and notify decisions by May 31.

7

Accept Offer & Prepare for India

If selected, Indian Embassy will allocate scholarship and send offer letter (by June 15). Accept offer by June 22! Then apply for Indian student visa, arrange accommodation (check if university provides hostel), book flight (one-way covered by scholarship), and prepare for departure in July-August!

📌 Critical Application Tips:

  • Attestation First: Get documents attested in February BEFORE portal opens - this is the most time-consuming step!
  • Apply to 5 Universities: Maximize chances by selecting 5 different universities/programmes in preference order
  • Match Subjects: Choose programmes matching your previous studies - rejection rate is high for mismatched applications
  • Contact Indian Embassy: Visit Indian Embassy in Yangon for guidance - they're very helpful!
  • 36 Slots Only: Myanmar gets 36 scholarships - competition exists, so make strong application!
04

Pro Tips for Success

📝

Document Attestation is Make-or-Break

Start attestation process in January/early February! Go to Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Myanmar with original documents. Process takes 2-4 weeks and can be delayed during holidays. Without attested documents, your application will be REJECTED regardless of merit. Don't underestimate this step!

🎯

Strategic University Selection

You get 5 choices - use them wisely! Include mix of: 1-2 top universities (IITs/NITs/Delhi), 2-3 good state universities (higher acceptance), 1 backup option. Research admission requirements carefully - some IITs have extremely high standards. Ensure your subjects match programme requirements!

🇮🇳

Contact Indian Embassy Early

Visit Indian Embassy in Yangon or check their website for latest information. Embassy staff can guide on: document requirements, popular universities among Myanmar students, application process, visa procedures. Many Myanmar students succeed through ICCR - learn from their experiences!

💡

Demonstrate India Connection

In statement of purpose, explain WHY India specifically! Mention: cultural ties between Myanmar and India, specific professors/research you admire, how Indian education fits your career goals, your interest in India-Myanmar relations. Show genuine enthusiasm beyond just "free scholarship"!

🔍

AIU Equivalence for Graduate Studies

If applying for Master's/PhD with Myanmar degrees, get AIU (Association of Indian Universities) Equivalence Certificate BEFORE applying! Universities require this to process your admission. Apply online at aiuweb.org - process takes 2-4 weeks. Without it, even scholarship offer cannot be processed!

📚

Match Academic Background to Programme

Carefully choose subjects based on your previous studies! Science student → Engineering/Science programmes. Commerce student → Business/Economics. Arts student → Humanities/Social Sciences. Cross-stream applications are almost always rejected. Check programme prerequisites on university websites!

🗣️

English Proficiency is Key

All programmes taught in English! Even if IELTS/TOEFL not required, write excellent 500-word essay. Practice English extensively before applying. Poor English = rejection even with good grades. If weak in English, consider taking English course before applying. Indian universities expect functional English proficiency!

💰

Budget Beyond Scholarship

Scholarship covers tuition + stipend (₹18,000-25,000/month) + accommodation allowance. This is SUFFICIENT for living in India! However, budget for: initial expenses (first month before stipend), textbooks, health insurance (~₹5,000-10,000/year), return airfare home (scholarship only covers one-way). Save $500-800 for settling-in period.
