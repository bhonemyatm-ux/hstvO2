Scholarship Overview

Child's Dream Foundation is a non-profit organization established in 2003, dedicated to providing access to education and healthcare for underprivileged children and youth in the Mekong Sub-Region (Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos, and Thailand). The University Scholarship Programme (USP), running since 2006, provides comprehensive scholarship support for academically strong students from financially disadvantaged backgrounds to pursue bachelor's degrees at reputable universities. The programme prioritizes students who demonstrate potential to create positive multiplier effects in their communities and contribute to sustainable development in their home countries.

The Child's Dream University Scholarship covers 100% of ALL student funding needs for the entire duration of study (typically 4 years) - including tuition fees, university fees, visa fees, living costs (rent, food, transportation), supplies (books, uniforms, stationery), health insurance, computer grant, graduation fees, and even emergency expenses on a case-by-case basis. Myanmar students can study at selected international programmes in Thailand (taught in English) or at universities in Myanmar. Since 2006, Child's Dream has supported hundreds of underprivileged students to achieve their educational dreams and become community leaders.

✨

Why Choose Child's Dream Scholarship?

This is PERFECT for financially disadvantaged Myanmar students! Child's Dream provides COMPLETE 100% funding - literally EVERYTHING: tuition + rent + food + transportation + books + uniforms + computer + health insurance + graduation + emergencies. You pay NOTHING! Study at excellent English-taught international programmes in Thailand (Mae Fah Luang, Thammasat, Mahidol) OR in Myanmar. The foundation truly CARES about students - personal support, mentorship, community of scholars. Age limit is generous (30 years), so even mature students can apply. Focus on FINANCIAL NEED means hardworking students from poor families have real chance! Since 2006, proven track record of changing lives!

03

How to Apply

1

Verify Eligibility - Financial Need is Key!

FIRST: Honestly assess if you meet financial need requirement. This scholarship is for students from poor families who CANNOT afford university. If your family can pay for education, please don't apply - save opportunity for truly needy students. Check age (≤30), nationality (Myanmar), education (12 years), no other scholarship.

2

Research Universities & Programmes

Review eligible programmes: Mae Fah Luang (Environmental Health, International Development), Thammasat (Engineering, Social Policy, Politics), or Myanmar universities. Consider which field matches your interests and how it will help you serve Myanmar community. Research university admission requirements carefully!

3

Gather Required Documents

Collect: Recent passport photo (< 6 months), National ID or Birth Certificate, passport (if available), academic certificates/transcripts, matriculation results. Organize these as PDFs for upload. Get passport photo taken professionally if needed.

4

Write Compelling Personal Essay

THIS IS CRITICAL! Write honest, heartfelt essay explaining: (1) Your family's financial situation and why you need support, (2) Your academic achievements despite challenges, (3) Why you want to study chosen field, (4) How you will use education to help Myanmar/your community. Be specific, genuine, and demonstrate commitment to social contribution!

5

Complete Online Application Form

Visit provided Google Form link (announced on Child's Dream Facebook page). Fill complete form carefully with accurate information. Upload all required documents. Write detailed responses to essay questions. Double-check everything before submission. Submit before January 30, 2026 deadline!

Visit Child's Dream Facebook →
6

Wait for Screening Results

Child's Dream team screens all applications (February-March). If shortlisted for interview, you'll be contacted by end of March. If you DON'T hear by end of March, your application was not successful this round. Don't lose hope - you can reapply next year if still eligible!

7

Prepare for Interview (If Shortlisted)

If invited to April interview: Prepare to discuss your background, financial situation, academic goals, motivation, community contribution plans. Be honest, passionate, and demonstrate commitment. Interview is face-to-face (location announced to shortlisted candidates). Dress professionally, arrive early, be yourself!

8

Apply to University (If Selected)

If you receive scholarship offer (mid-May): Sign grant letter and budget agreement with Child's Dream. Then apply to chosen university (May-July) - this is YOUR responsibility but Child's Dream provides guidance. Meet university admission requirements, sit entrance exams if needed. Get admission, then prepare to start studies August 2026!

📌 Critical Application Tips:

  • Honesty is Everything: Be completely honest about financial situation - Child's Dream can verify information
  • Personal Essay Quality: Spend significant time on essay - this is how you stand out!
  • Demonstrate Need AND Merit: Show both why you need support and why you deserve it (academic achievements, community work, leadership)
  • Community Impact Focus: Emphasize how you'll use education to help Myanmar - align with Child's Dream mission
  • Apply Early: Don't wait until deadline - technical issues can happen!
04

Pro Tips for Success

💰

Financial Need is PRIMARY Criterion

Child's Dream PRIORITIZES financially disadvantaged students. In your essay, clearly explain family's economic situation: parents' income/occupation, number of siblings, financial challenges. Be specific but respectful. Demonstrate that WITHOUT this scholarship, university is impossible. Students from poor families have BEST chance - don't be shy about financial struggles!

📝

Personal Essay Must Tell YOUR Story

Avoid generic essays! Tell YOUR unique story: challenges you overcame, why education matters to YOU, specific experiences that shaped your goals. Child's Dream reads hundreds of applications - make yours memorable! Show personality, passion, resilience. Use concrete examples: "My father is a farmer earning X/month supporting 6 children..." Better than vague statements!

🌏

Demonstrate Community Commitment

Child's Dream seeks students who will create "positive multiplier effect" in communities! Show your community service, volunteer work, helping others despite own hardships. Explain specific plans: "After studying Environmental Health, I'll return to my village to improve water sanitation..." Foundation invests in future community leaders!

🎓

Academic Merit Still Matters

While financial need is primary, you must show academic capability! Highlight good grades, academic achievements, awards, exam scores. If grades are average, explain challenges you overcame (working while studying, family responsibilities, etc.). Demonstrate you're academically prepared for university despite disadvantages!

🇹🇭

Thailand vs Myanmar Decision

Thailand programmes offer: International exposure, English proficiency, modern facilities, networking across Southeast Asia. Myanmar programmes offer: Stay with family, familiar environment, direct community impact during studies, lower cultural adjustment. Choose based on YOUR priorities and family situation. Both are excellent options!

🗣️

English Proficiency Preparation

For Thailand programmes, you'll be assessed on English! Practice speaking English now - watch English videos, practice with friends, take free online courses. During interview (if shortlisted), you may need to demonstrate English ability. Don't let language be barrier - start improving today! Even basic fluency helps!

💡

Interview Preparation Strategy

If shortlisted for April interview: Prepare answers to likely questions (Why this field? How will you help Myanmar? Tell us about yourself? What are your strengths/weaknesses?). Practice with friends/family. Be yourself - honesty and genuine passion matter more than perfect answers. Dress modestly and professionally. Show gratitude for opportunity!

📅

University Application Timeline

IMPORTANT: You apply to university AFTER getting scholarship (May-July), not before! But research university requirements NOW so you know what's needed. Some universities have entrance exams - find out early. Mae Fah Luang and Thammasat have different deadlines and processes. Being scholarship recipient helps admission but you must still meet requirements!
