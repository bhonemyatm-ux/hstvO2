About IAL Heho

A practical agricultural training center serving the Shan Highlands

The Institute of Agriculture and Livestock (Heho) is a government-run agricultural diploma institute situated in Kalaw Township, Shan State. Located in the cool highlands of the Shan plateau, it offers practical diploma courses focused on highland crops, livestock raising, and plantation management.

Unlike large universities, IAL Heho focuses on producing job-ready agricultural technicians. Around 70% of the curriculum is hands-on farm work across its 100+ acre training campus — making it one of the most practical agriculture programs in the country.

The institute is under the Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock & Irrigation and produces graduates who are highly valued in rural development, government extension services, and commercial farming.

Agricultural Diploma Programs

2–3 year courses • 70% practical training on farm campus

🌾
Crop Production
Diploma — 2 Years

Covers cultivation of highland crops, rice, vegetables, and organic farming techniques. Students work directly on the institute's research farm throughout the course.

🌾 Rice Cultivation 🥬 Vegetable Farming 🌱 Organic Methods 💧 Irrigation Systems 🧪 Soil Science 🌡️ Climate Adaptation
🐄
Livestock Management
Diploma — 2 Years

Focuses on animal husbandry, feed management, and basic veterinary care. Covers cattle, poultry, and piggery operations using the institute's dedicated livestock center.

🐄 Cattle Husbandry 🐔 Poultry Farming 🐖 Piggery Operations 🏥 Veterinary Basics 🌾 Feed Management 📋 Animal Health Records
🌳
Forestry & Horticulture
Diploma — 2 Years

Covers tea and coffee cultivation, fruit orchards, and plantation management — skills uniquely suited to the cool Shan highland environment that most agricultural institutes cannot replicate.

🍵 Tea Plantation ☕ Coffee Cultivation 🍎 Fruit Orchards 🌲 Plantation Management 🌱 Tree Nursery 📦 Post-Harvest Handling

ℹ️ A note for students: IAL Heho is a lower-level diploma institute — it does not award bachelor's degrees. However, graduates often join extension services, cooperatives, or continue to degree-level agriculture at universities like Yezin Agricultural University with added experience. This is a practical, career-first track.

What to expect: Life at IAL Heho is rural and practical. Students live on or near campus, wake up early for farm duties, and spend most of the day outdoors. If you are passionate about agriculture and prefer doing over sitting in lectures, this is the environment for you. The cool Shan highland climate is a bonus — no sweltering heat.

Honest career outlook: IAL Heho graduates do not typically enter high-paying corporate careers. But in a country where most people depend on agriculture, trained agricultural technicians are genuinely valuable. Government jobs, NGO agricultural projects, and commercial farms all actively hire diploma holders. If you want to work in Shan State and contribute to rural development — this is one of the most direct paths.
