Scholarship Overview

The Asian Institute of Technology (AIT) is Asia's premier international postgraduate institution, located in Bangkok, Thailand. Established in 1959, AIT offers world-class Master's and Doctoral programmes in Engineering, Environment, Management, and Information and Communications Technologies. AIT provides multiple scholarship opportunities for outstanding Myanmar students, including AIT Scholarships (50-100% tuition waiver), Royal Thai Government Scholarships (H.M. the King's and H.M. the Queen's), and specialized scholarships like KAAD-AIT for Catholic students.

AIT scholarships are highly accessible compared to European/American programmes, with lower English requirements (IELTS 5.0-6.0), proximity to Myanmar (3-hour flight), affordable living costs (₿11,500-15,000/month), and strong regional networking opportunities. The institute operates entirely in English with students from 40+ countries, offering excellent research facilities and strong industry connections across Asia. Graduates enjoy high employment rates in international organizations, NGOs, government agencies, and private sectors throughout Asia-Pacific.

✨

Why Choose AIT?

AIT is PERFECT for Myanmar students seeking international graduate education! Unlike distant Western universities, AIT is just 3 hours away with affordable living costs (₿11,500-15,000/month = ~$350-450 USD). English requirements are reasonable (IELTS 5.0-6.0), making it more accessible. Multiple scholarship options (50-100% tuition) + possible living allowances! Campus is truly international with 40+ nationalities, creating valuable Asian networks. Programmes are practice-oriented with strong industry partnerships. NO TOEFL/GRE required! Plus, warm climate similar to Myanmar and Buddhist culture make adaptation easier!

Scholarship Details

📋

Types of AIT Scholarships

Multiple Scholarship Opportunities Available:

  • AIT Scholarships: 50-100% tuition fee waiver (main scholarship for all nationalities)
  • H.M. the King's Scholarships: Full tuition + living allowance (priority for ASEAN, especially Myanmar/Cambodia/Laos/Vietnam/Timor-Leste)
  • H.M. the Queen's Scholarships: Full tuition + living allowance (priority for ASEAN countries)
  • Greater Mekong Subregion (GMS) Scholarships: For Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Vietnam, Thailand, China
  • KAAD-AIT Scholarships: Full funding for Catholic students from Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Vietnam, Timor-Leste
  • DAAD Scholarships at AIT: For Southeast Asian students (Myanmar included)
🏛️

Host Institution

Asian Institute of Technology (AIT)

International postgraduate institution in Pathum Thani (north of Bangkok), Thailand. Established 1959, AIT is Asia's oldest international engineering school with autonomous charter. Offers only graduate programmes (no undergraduate). Students from 40+ countries create truly multicultural environment. Medium of instruction: 100% English. Strong focus on sustainable development, technology innovation, and capacity building in Asia-Pacific region.

03

How to Apply

1

Research AIT Programmes & Scholarships

Visit AIT website to explore programmes across four schools (Engineering, Environment, Management, Advanced Technologies). Identify programmes matching your background and interests. Review different scholarship options: AIT Scholarships (all nationalities), Royal Thai Government (ASEAN priority), KAAD-AIT (Catholic students).

AIT Admissions →
2

Take English Proficiency Test

Take IELTS Academic (aim for 5.0-6.0) OR save money by taking FREE AIT English Entry Test (AIT EET) online or on campus! AIT EET is accepted and costs nothing compared to IELTS ($220). Check AIT Language Center website for EET schedule.

3

Prepare Academic Documents

Gather official transcripts from all universities, degree certificates, two recommendation letters from professors/employers. Have documents certified if required. Prepare detailed CV highlighting academic achievements, research experience, work experience, publications if any.

4

Write Statement of Purpose/Research Proposal

Craft compelling statement explaining: Why AIT? Why this programme? Your research interests? Career goals? How will studies contribute to Myanmar's development? For PhD, include detailed research proposal with clear objectives, methodology, expected outcomes. Be specific and passionate!

5

Submit Online Application

Create account on AIT admissions portal. Complete online application form with accurate information. Upload all required documents (transcripts, certificates, recommendations, English test, statement of purpose, passport). Indicate scholarship interest clearly!

Apply Online →
6

Apply for Royal Thai Government Scholarships (Optional but Recommended)

If eligible, ALSO apply separately for H.M. King's/Queen's/GMS scholarships (deadline Feb 28). These offer FULL funding including living allowance! Application process similar but separate from regular AIT admission. Check AIT website for specific procedures.

7

Await Results & Accept Offer

AIT reviews applications within 6-8 weeks. If accepted, you'll receive admission letter specifying scholarship award (50%, 75%, or 100% tuition waiver). Accept offer by deadline, complete enrollment procedures, apply for Thai student visa, arrange accommodation (on-campus recommended), and prepare for departure!

📌 Important Application Tips:

  • Two Intakes: August (main intake, more scholarships) and January - apply for intake that fits your schedule
  • Early Application: Earlier applicants have better scholarship chances - don't wait until deadline!
  • Multiple Scholarships: Apply for AIT admission (auto-considered for AIT Scholarships) + separately for Royal Thai Government scholarships
  • Contact AIT: Email admissions@ait.ac.th with questions - they're very responsive!
  • NO Application Fee: AIT doesn't charge application fees!
04

Pro Tips for Success

💰

Target Royal Thai Government Scholarships

Myanmar students have PRIORITY for H.M. King's and Queen's scholarships! These are FULL funding (100% tuition + living allowance + accommodation). Apply separately by Feb 28 deadline. Even if you don't get Royal Thai scholarship, you'll still be considered for regular AIT scholarships!

🗣️

Use AIT English Test to Save Money

IELTS costs $220 and requires travel to test center. AIT offers FREE English Entry Test (AIT EET) online or on campus, which is fully accepted! If you're confident in English, take AIT EET first. Only take IELTS if you need it for other applications too. This saves significant money!

📊

Highlight Development Impact

AIT focuses on sustainable development in Asia-Pacific. In your statement of purpose, clearly explain how your studies will contribute to Myanmar's development challenges (infrastructure, environment, agriculture, technology, etc.). Show specific plans to apply learning back home!

🎓

GPA Matters - But Not Everything

Minimum GPA is 2.75-3.0, but higher GPA improves scholarship chances. If GPA is borderline, strengthen application with strong research proposal, relevant work experience, publications, clear motivation. AIT uses holistic evaluation - not just numbers!

👥

Connect with Myanmar Alumni

Many Myanmar students have studied at AIT! Reach out to alumni through Facebook groups or LinkedIn. Ask about: scholarship experiences, programme quality, living in Thailand, budget planning. Their insights are invaluable! AIT also has active Myanmar Students Association on campus.

📝

Strong Recommendation Letters Are Critical

Get recommendations from professors/supervisors who KNOW you well and can speak to your academic abilities, research potential, and character. Generic letters don't help! Provide recommenders with your CV, research interests, why AIT, so they can write detailed, specific letters.

🏠

Budget Even with Scholarship

If you get 50% tuition waiver, budget for: remaining tuition (~₿125,000/year), registration fees (₿20,000/semester), accommodation (₿2,000-4,000/month), living expenses (₿11,500-15,000/month). Total: ~₿400,000-500,000/year (~$12,000-15,000). Still very affordable compared to Western universities!

⏰

Apply Early for August Intake

August intake has MORE scholarship slots and better funding opportunities than January. Plus, starting in August aligns with most academic calendars. If possible, target August intake with June 10 deadline. But if you miss it, January intake is still good!
