What is a Scholarship?

The Journey Begins
Free Money for Your Education

A scholarship is FREE MONEY for your education. You never have to pay it back — it's a gift from governments, universities, or organisations to help talented, hardworking students like you pursue higher education.

💡
Think of it this way

"I see how smart and determined you are. I want to pay for your university education — tuition, books, housing, living expenses — everything. You just focus on learning!"

That's exactly what a scholarship is.

Why Do Countries Give Scholarships?

"Why would another country pay for MY education?" Great question — here's why:

Building Bridges Between Nations
Shaping Tomorrow's Leaders
🤝
Building Friendships
Countries invest in students to build long-term relationships. When you study there, you become a bridge between two nations.
🌟
Attracting Talent
Universities want the brightest students worldwide. Your perspective and determination make their campus richer.
💼
Future Leaders
They invest in you today knowing you'll become a leader who drives positive change in your country tomorrow.
🌏
Regional Growth
ASEAN and Asian nations support each other's development through education and knowledge exchange.
🇲🇲
For Myanmar Students Specifically

Many countries recognise Myanmar's potential and want to help develop our nation's next leaders. When you return with a world-class education, you'll contribute to Myanmar's growth in medicine, technology, business, and beyond.

Section 03

Types of Scholarships

There are many types of scholarships. Here are the four main categories:

1

Government Scholarships

Funded by foreign governments to bring international students to their country. Usually fully funded.

ASEAN (Singapore) Chevening (UK) DAAD (Germany) MEXT (Japan) Full Funding
2

University Scholarships

Offered directly by universities to attract talented students. Coverage varies.

NUS Global Merit NTU Scholarship Harvard Partial–Full
3

Organisation / Foundation Scholarships

From non-profits, companies, or charitable foundations — often substantial.

Fulbright (USA) MasterCard Foundation ADB-Japan Usually Full
4

Regional Scholarships (ASEAN / Asia)

Designed specifically for ASEAN or Asian students — culturally closer to Myanmar.

ASEAN Scholarship Thai Gov. Scholarship Full Funding
✨
Good News!

You can apply to multiple scholarships at the same time — there's no limit. Many successful students apply to 10–15 scholarships to maximise their chances!

Can I Really Apply?

YES — if you're reading this, you can apply. Let's bust the common myths Myanmar students believe:

Students from every corner of Myanmar have done it.
✕
"My English isn't perfect"
Many scholarships offer language prep courses! IELTS 6.0–6.5 is often enough to start.
✕
"My grades aren't straight As"
Many scholarships accept 70%+. Leadership, character, and your story matter just as much.
✕
"I'm from a small town, not Yangon"
Students from Taunggyi, Mawlamyine, and Myitkyina have won international scholarships!
✕
"My family can't afford application fees"
Most scholarships have ZERO application fees.
✕
"I don't know anyone who got a scholarship"
You could be the first in your community. Someone has to start — why not you?

Section 05

How Do I Apply?

We'll guide you through every step. Here's the complete application process:

1
Step 1 — 1–2 months

Research & Choose Scholarships

  • Browse our scholarship database on this website
  • Identify 10–15 scholarships matching your interests
  • Note all deadlines in a calendar
  • Read each scholarship's requirements carefully
2
Step 2 — 2–3 months

Prepare Your Documents

  • Get certified copies of your academic transcripts
  • Gather certificates and awards
  • Register for the IELTS / TOEFL exam
  • Request recommendation letters from teachers (at least 1 month early!)
  • Start drafting your personal statement
3
Step 3 — 1–2 months

Write Your Essays

  • Write YOUR unique story — not someone else's template
  • Explain your goals and how the scholarship helps achieve them
  • Connect your education to Myanmar's future
  • Have teachers or mentors review your drafts
  • Revise 3–5 times (that's completely normal!)
4
Step 4

Submit Your Applications

  • Most scholarships accept online applications
  • Double-check every document before submitting
  • Submit at least 1 week before the deadline
  • Save screenshots and confirmation emails
5
Step 5 — 2–6 months wait

Wait for Results

  • Check your email regularly (including the spam folder!)
  • Prepare for possible interviews
  • Stay patient — selection takes time
  • Keep studying and applying to other scholarships
