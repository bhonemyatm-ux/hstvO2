About Myanmar Maritime University

Myanmar Maritime University (MMU) is Myanmar's only university dedicated entirely to maritime and naval disciplines. Established in 2002 under the Myanmar Maritime University Act (State Peace and Development Council Law No. 1/2002), it was built to produce degree-qualified engineers, naval architects, and nautical science officers for Myanmar's maritime, port, and coastal sectors.

The university occupies a purpose-built campus in Thanlyin, near Thilawa Port on the outskirts of Yangon — placing students in direct proximity to one of Myanmar's most significant port and industrial zones. This location gives students real-world industry exposure throughout their studies.

MMU operates under the Ministry of Transport and Communications and aligns all programs with international STCW-95 standards set by the International Maritime Organization (IMO). It is widely regarded as one of Myanmar's most selective universities, with graduates placed in shipyards, shipping companies, port authorities, and offshore engineering roles both domestically and internationally.

STCW-95 Aligned — IMO International Maritime Training Framework

Why Choose MMU?

Only Maritime University in Myanmar

MMU is Myanmar's sole university offering full engineering and science degrees in maritime, naval, and coastal disciplines — making it irreplaceable for serious maritime careers.

International Standards

All programs are aligned with STCW-95 (IMO framework), ensuring graduates meet the international standards required for seafaring and maritime engineering careers globally.

Strategic Campus Location

Situated near Thilawa Port and Special Economic Zone — students benefit from proximity to Myanmar's most active maritime and industrial hub throughout their studies.

High Graduate Placement

Known for strong placement of graduates in international shipping, offshore engineering, port authorities, and ship design firms — both within Myanmar and abroad.

Broad Engineering Scope

From naval architecture and coastal engineering to marine electrical systems — MMU's seven departments cover the full spectrum of maritime and marine engineering disciplines.

Postgraduate Pathways

Since 2012, MMU offers two-year postgraduate diplomas for professionals seeking advanced specialization in marine and naval disciplines beyond the undergraduate degree.

Historical Milestones

2002
Myanmar Maritime University established under the Myanmar Maritime University Act — SPDC Law No. 1/2002. The first and only maritime-focused degree-granting university in Myanmar.
Early
Purpose-built campus opened in Thanlyin, near Thilawa Port — strategically positioned to give students and faculty direct proximity to Myanmar's foremost maritime industrial zone.
2012
Postgraduate diploma programs launched — two-year post-graduate diplomas in marine and naval disciplines introduced, expanding MMU's academic offering beyond the undergraduate level.
Now
MMU continues to offer 7 undergraduate departments awarding B.E. and B.Sc. (Hons) degrees, fully aligned with STCW-95 international standards, producing maritime engineers and officers for domestic and international careers.
