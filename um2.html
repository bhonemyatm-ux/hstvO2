About the University

ဆေးတက္ကသိုလ် (၂) ရန်ကုန်သည် မြန်မာနိုင်ငံ၏ ထင်ရှားသောဆေးပညာသင်ကြားရေးဌာနများထဲမှ တစ်ခုဖြစ်ပြီး ၁၉၆၃ ခုနှစ် ဇူလိုင်လ ၁၅ ရက်နေ့တွင် Medical College 2 အဖြစ် မင်္ဂလာဒုံမြို့နယ်တွင် တည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပါသည်။ Education ဝန်ကြီးဌာနလက်အောက်တွင် ရန်ကုန်တက္ကသိုလ်နှင့် ဆက်စပ်အဖြစ် စတင်ကာ ၁၉၆၄ ခုနှစ်တွင် Institute of Medicine 2 အဖြစ် ကိုယ်တိုင်လွတ်လပ်သောတက္ကသိုလ်ဖြစ်လာခဲ့သည်။

University of Medicine 2, Yangon (UM2) was established on 15 July 1963 as Medical College 2 in Mingaladon Township, affiliated with Yangon University under the Ministry of Education. In 1964 it became an independent university, and in 1973 it was transferred to the Ministry of Health. In 2005, it was officially renamed University of Medicine 2, Yangon.

Today, UM2 stands as one of Myanmar's most selective universities, accepting only around 300 students per year — an acceptance rate of approximately 10% — based solely on University Entrance Examination scores. Recognized by ECFMG, WHO, and the Myanmar Medical Council, UM2's mission is clear: "To produce competent basic doctors and highly qualified post-graduate physicians."

Historical Milestones

1963
Medical College 2 established on 15 July in Mingaladon Township, affiliated with Yangon University under the Ministry of Education.
1964
Became Institute of Medicine 2 — an independent university under the University Act of 1964.
1970
North Okkalapa General Hospital and Insein General Hospital became affiliated teaching hospitals, greatly expanding clinical training capacity.
1973
Transferred to the Ministry of Health (from Ministry of Education) under the Department of Medical Education on 1 October.
2005
Officially renamed University of Medicine 2, Yangon — its current name.

Curriculum Reform

New Integrated Curriculum (NIC)

UM2 has adopted a fully integrated, system-based M.B.,B.S. curriculum replacing the traditional subject-siloed approach. The NIC introduces junior clerkship from Medical Year 3, giving students real patient contact and clinical reasoning skills years earlier than conventional models.

Integrated Learning Clinical Clerkship System-Based Modules
Ongoing
International Recognition

Triple Accreditation Maintenance

UM2 maintains accreditation from ECFMG (USA), WHO, and the Myanmar Medical Council — one of only a handful of Myanmar institutions with this triple recognition. This enables graduates to pursue USMLE examinations and international residency applications.

ECFMG WHO Myanmar Medical Council

Postgraduate Research Programs
