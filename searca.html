Scholarship Overview

The Southeast Asian Regional Center for Graduate Study and Research in Agriculture (SEARCA) is a premier regional center under the Southeast Asian Ministers of Education Organization (SEAMEO). Established in 1966 and headquartered at the University of the Philippines Los Baños (UPLB), SEARCA has been providing fully-funded graduate scholarships for over 55 years, supporting agricultural development and food security across Southeast Asia. Since its inception, SEARCA has granted 1,991 full Master's and PhD scholarships to nationals of SEAMEO member countries, creating a network of agricultural leaders throughout the region.

The SEARCA Graduate Scholarship supports highly qualified individuals from Myanmar and other SEAMEO countries to pursue Master's and PhD degrees in agriculture and allied sciences at accredited universities across Southeast Asia. The scholarship is FULLY FUNDED - covering tuition fees, monthly living stipend, travel allowance, health insurance, thesis/dissertation support, and book allowance. Research focus areas align with SEARCA's strategic thrusts: increasing farmers' income, improving food security and nutrition, enhancing climate resilience of agricultural systems, and empowering agricultural stakeholders. Myanmar students can study at top regional universities in the Philippines, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

✨

Why Choose SEARCA Scholarship?

SEARCA is PERFECT for Myanmar students in agriculture! It's a REGIONAL scholarship (Southeast Asia focus) with strong Myanmar connection. You get COMPLETE funding: full tuition + monthly stipend + travel + health insurance + thesis support + books. Study at excellent universities within Southeast Asia - culturally familiar, affordable living, similar climate! Your employer continues paying salary during studies (if government employee)! Research directly benefits Myanmar agriculture - rice, livestock, climate adaptation, food security. Over 55 years of experience, 1,991+ scholars, strong alumni network. Plus, studies are in ENGLISH at most universities!

03

How to Apply

1

Verify Employment Eligibility

FIRST CHECK: Are you employed by academic institution, research institute, or government agency? If YES, proceed. If NO (fresh graduate), you must first secure employment to be eligible. Get Certificate of Employment from your employer on official letterhead stating position, employment status (permanent/regular), and duration of service.

2

Develop Research Proposal

THIS IS CRITICAL! Write detailed thesis/dissertation proposal (3-5 pages). Must align with SEARCA priorities: farmers' income, food security/nutrition, climate resilience, OR empowered stakeholders. Choose topic relevant to Myanmar agriculture (e.g., rice production, livestock systems, climate adaptation, agricultural extension). Research universities' faculty expertise to match your topic!

3

Research Universities & Programs

Visit www.searca.org to view accredited universities. Popular choices: UPLB (Philippines - SEARCA HQ), Kasetsart (Thailand), UPM (Malaysia), IPB (Indonesia). Check which universities offer programmes in your specialization. Consider location, language, faculty expertise, living costs. Can apply to multiple universities in preference order!

View Accredited Universities →
4

Gather Required Documents

Collect: Official transcripts from all universities, copies of all degrees/diplomas, passport, recent photo, Certificate of Employment, Medical Certificate (from licensed physician), CV/resume. Request 2-3 recommendation letters from professors or supervisors (give them 3-4 weeks notice). Organize everything before starting online application!

5

Complete Online Application

When portal opens (July 2026), register at www.searca.org/scholarships. Fill complete online form carefully with accurate information. Upload all required documents as PDFs. Submit detailed research proposal. Select preferred universities in order. Review everything thoroughly before final submission. Download/print confirmation and complete application package!

6

Submit to Myanmar Ministry of Education

CRITICAL STEP! Print complete application with all supporting documents. Submit physical copy to Ministry of Education/Ministry of Higher Education in Myanmar for endorsement. Ministry reviews and forwards endorsed applications to SEARCA. Without Ministry endorsement, your application will NOT be considered! Check Ministry office for specific submission procedures.

7

Prepare for Interview & Await Results

If shortlisted (November-December), SEARCA will contact you for interview (virtual or in-person). Prepare to discuss research proposal, motivation, career goals, commitment to Myanmar agriculture. Results announced January-February 2027. If selected, sign scholarship contract, arrange leave from employer, prepare for departure June-August 2027!

📌 Critical Success Tips:

  • Employment is Mandatory: Fresh graduates must get job first at university, research institute, or government before applying
  • Research Proposal Quality: Spend significant time developing strong proposal - this is main selection criterion!
  • SEARCA Priority Alignment: Clearly show how research contributes to farmers' income, food security, climate resilience, or empowerment
  • Myanmar Relevance: Emphasize how study will benefit Myanmar agriculture and your contribution after graduation
  • Ministry Endorsement: Don't forget physical submission to Ministry - online alone is NOT sufficient!
04

Pro Tips for Success

🔬

Research Proposal is Everything

Selection is HEAVILY based on research proposal quality! Be specific: clear objectives, sound methodology, practical applications for Myanmar. Cite relevant literature. Show innovation. Explain why this research matters for Myanmar farmers/food security. Get feedback from senior colleagues before submitting. Weak proposal = rejection even with good grades!

💼

Employment Requirement Strategy

Fresh graduates: Target positions at: Yezin Agricultural University, Department of Agriculture, Department of Agricultural Research, livestock breeding stations, agricultural research institutes. Even short-term employment counts if it's regular/permanent status. Government employees have advantage (salary continues during scholarship!). Get employment letter that clearly states "permanent" or "regular" status!

🇵🇭

University Selection Matters

UPLB (Philippines) is popular - SEARCA HQ is there, strong Myanmar student community, English-speaking, affordable. Kasetsart (Thailand) - close to Myanmar, great facilities, Buddhist culture. UPM (Malaysia) - modern, diverse, good stipend. IPB (Indonesia) - top agricultural research, tropical focus. Choose based on specialization strength + comfort level + language!

📝

Link Research to Myanmar Development

SEARCA wants scholars who will return and contribute! Clearly explain: How will your research help Myanmar farmers? What problem are you solving? How will you apply knowledge after graduation? Specific examples: "My research on drought-resistant rice varieties will help Dry Zone farmers adapt to climate change and increase yields by X%". Show commitment to Myanmar!

🏛️

Ministry Endorsement Process

Visit Ministry of Education or Ministry of Higher Education in Naypyidaw/Yangon EARLY to understand endorsement process. Bring: printed online application, all supporting documents, cover letter explaining scholarship. Ministry staff can guide on requirements. Building good relationship with Ministry helps! Some applicants visit multiple times to ensure proper submission.

🌾

Focus on Priority Areas

SEARCA's 4 priorities: (1) Farmers' income - value chains, market access, (2) Food security/nutrition - crop production, food systems, (3) Climate resilience - adaptation strategies, resilient varieties, (4) Stakeholder empowerment - extension, farmer organizations. Pick ONE and align your research clearly. Don't try to cover everything - be focused!

💰

Salary Continuation Advantage

Government employees: Your agency continues paying salary during scholarship! This means you get BOTH monthly salary AND scholarship stipend = very comfortable financially! Confirm this with HR before applying. Private institution employees: negotiate study leave with salary or unpaid leave. Some research institutes also continue salary for scholarship recipients!

🤝

Network with SEARCA Alumni

Myanmar has many SEARCA alumni! Find them at agricultural universities, Department of Agriculture, research institutes. Ask about: application process, university experiences, research tips, living abroad, career impact. Alumni recommendations carry weight. Join "SEARCA Scholars Myanmar" network if exists. Their insights are invaluable for strong application!
