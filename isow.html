Scholarship Overview

The ISOW (International Students Overcoming War) Scholarship at Wilfrid Laurier University is a unique student-led, student-funded initiative providing full financial support to undergraduate and graduate students from conflict-affected regions, including Myanmar, to study at Laurier in Waterloo and Brantford campuses.[3][1]

Funded by $8/semester contributions from all Laurier students plus university and NGO partners, it covers tuition, living expenses, textbooks, health insurance, and more - total value exceeding $50,000 per year. Scholars engage in experiential learning, leadership development, and community building to create positive change back home.[2][3]

Why ISOW Scholarship Stands Out?

100% student-funded model demonstrates genuine peer support; comprehensive coverage removes all barriers; focus on conflict-affected students like those from Myanmar; strong mentorship from ISOW leaders; no service bond - freedom to return home immediately after studies.
