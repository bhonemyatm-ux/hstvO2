Academic Programs

Undergraduate and postgraduate programmes in arts, sciences and law

📚

Arts Programmes

Bachelor of Arts (B.A.)

Arts degrees offered through departments such as Myanmar, English, History, Philosophy, Psychology, Anthropology and International Relations.

Duration: Standard Undergraduate Length
Min. Marks: Matriculation aggregate meeting BA cut-off
Prerequisites: Relevant arts combination in matriculation
Myanmar English History International Relations
🧪

Science Programmes

Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.)

Science degrees through departments including Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Geology, Zoology and Biology.

Duration: Standard Undergraduate Length
Min. Marks: Matriculation aggregate meeting BSc cut-off
Prerequisites: Science combination at matriculation
Physics Chemistry Mathematics Geology & Zoology
⚖️

Law Programme

Bachelor of Laws (LL.B)

Law degree offered by the Department of Law, preparing graduates for legal practice, public service and further legal studies.

Duration: Standard Undergraduate Length
Min. Marks: Matriculation aggregate meeting law cut-off
Prerequisites: High matriculation score, law stream selection
Legal Theory Civil & Criminal Law International Law Constitutional Law
🎓

Postgraduate Degrees

Master's Programmes

Master's degrees in various arts, science and law subjects, including MA, MSc and LLM programmes.

Duration: At least 1 Year of Study
Min. Marks: Honours or qualifying exam with GPA 4 in each subject
Prerequisites: Relevant bachelor degree and selection
MA / MSc LLM Research Thesis Viva Voce Exam
📜

Doctorate & Diplomas

PhD & Postgraduate Diplomas

Doctoral programmes and postgraduate diplomas across selected arts and science disciplines.

Duration: Multi-year Research or Coursework
Min. Marks: Strong postgraduate performance
Prerequisites: Master's degree and approved proposal (for PhD)
PhD Research Specialized Diplomas Advanced Seminars Thesis Submission

Student Life at Taungoo University

Lakeside campus with hostels, libraries and green spaces

Hostel Life

Students stay on-campus in hostels, living side by side, studying, eating and spending time together in a shared community.

Green & Historic Campus

The campus is known for its greenery, lakeside views and iconic buildings, offering a calm environment especially during the rainy season.

Libraries & Study Spaces

Students use the university library and lecture halls for independent study, group work and classes as part of becoming autonomous learners.
