About the University

ဆေးတက္ကသိုလ် (တောင်ကြီး) သည် မြန်မာနိုင်ငံ၏ ငါးကြိမ်မြောက် ဆေးတက္ကသိုလ်ဖြစ်ပြီး ၂၀၁၅ ခုနှစ် အောက်တိုဘာလ ၃ ရက်နေ့တွင် တည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပါသည်။ ရှမ်းပြည်နယ်နှင့် ကယားပြည်နယ်တို့မှ ကျောင်းသားများကို ဆေးပညာသင်ကြားရေး အခွင့်အလမ်းများ တိုးချဲ့ပေးရန် ရည်ရွယ်ကာ တည်ထောင်ခဲ့သည်။

The University of Medicine, Taunggyi (UMTgi) was established on 3 October 2015, with its first enrollment of 200 students in December 2015 — drawn primarily from Shan State and Kayah State. It is one of only five medical universities in Myanmar, and the only one serving the vast highland regions of the country.

Situated on a 46-acre campus of 22 buildings along National Highway No. 4, approximately 3.4 km southeast of Taunggyi city centre, UMTgi was founded as part of Myanmar's ambitious 30-year healthcare policy — aiming to raise the doctor-to-population ratio from 1:3300 in 2000 to 10:1100 by 2030. Its motto, ဥပဌာနံ ၊ အနုကမ္မာ ၊ ဒယာ — Service, Sympathy, Humanity — reflects its founding commitment to compassionate, community-centred medicine.

Undergraduate Program

The sole medical degree offered — built for Shan State's future doctors

🩺

Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery

M.B.,B.S.

A 7-year program (including one year of house surgeon training) covering foundational sciences, system-based medical modules, and structured clinical rotations in Taunggyi's teaching hospitals. The curriculum is designed to produce competent, ethical doctors ready to serve Shan State and beyond.

Total Duration 7 Years (incl. 1 yr house surgeon)
Admission Basis Academic records & grades (STEAMS 1)

M.B.,B.S. Course Structure

7 years from foundation sciences to full clinical rotations

Year / Phase Duration Key Subjects

The Campus

A modern medical campus in the heart of Shan State

📍

Main Campus — Taunggyi

Located on National Highway No. 4 (Taunggyi–Hopone Road) and Shwe Phone Pwint Pagoda Road, approximately 3.4 km southeast of Taunggyi city centre. The campus spans 18.6 hectares (46 acres) and comprises 22 buildings.

What's on Campus

  • Foundation year departments: Burmese, English, Mathematics & Statistics, Chemistry, Physics, Botany, Zoology
  • Administrative offices: Administration, Finance & Accounting, Student Affairs, Foreign Relations
  • Examination office, Library, Sports facilities, Computer Science department
  • 22 buildings across 46 acres — one of Myanmar's most purpose-built medical campuses
