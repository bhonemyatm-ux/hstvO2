Overview

Chevening is the UK government's global scholarship programme, funded by the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office and partner organisations. Since 1983, nearly 60,000 professionals have used it to study in the UK and build lasting relationships with Britain.

The programme awards fully-funded one-year master's degrees to outstanding emerging leaders at any UK university. On average, thirteen Myanmar nationals receive a Chevening Scholarship each year and join an alumni network of more than 60,000 worldwide.

✦

What sets Chevening apart

Beyond the funding, Chevening offers entry into a global community — heads of state, business leaders, journalists, and changemakers among its alumni. Scholars receive a world-class British education, a structured professional development programme, and exclusive events that build lifelong networks across continents.

Eligibility

Basic requirements

  • ✓
    Citizenship. Must be a Myanmar citizen — not a UK or dual UK national.
  • ✓
    Return commitment. Must return to Myanmar for at least two years after your studies.
  • ✓
    No prior UK government funding. You must not have studied in the UK on a UK government scholarship before.

Academic requirements

  • ✓
    Bachelor's degree. Equivalent to a UK upper second-class (2:1) honours — typically 60% or higher.
  • ✓
    Three university choices. You must apply to exactly three different eligible UK master's courses.
  • ✓
    Programme start. All chosen courses must begin in autumn (September or October).

Work experience

  • ✓
    Minimum 2,800 hours. Roughly two years of full-time, part-time, voluntary, or internship work.
  • ✓
    Must be completed by the application deadline.

English language

  • ✓
    IELTS Academic. Overall 6.5 with no component below 6.0 (Chevening's minimum).
  • ✓
    Many universities require IELTS 7.0+ — check each programme separately.
!

Important. You cannot apply if you are an employee, former employee, or relative of an employee of the UK government (British Embassy / FCDO) or the Association of Commonwealth Universities.
