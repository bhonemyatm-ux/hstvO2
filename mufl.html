About the University

မန္တလေး နိုင်ငံခြား ဘာသာ တက္ကသိုလ် သည် အထက်မြန်မာပြည်၏ တစ်ခုတည်းသော နိုင်ငံခြားဘာသာသင်ကြားရေးတက္ကသိုလ် ဖြစ်ပါသည်။ ၁၉၉၇ ခုနှစ်တွင် တည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပြီး အထက်ပိုင်းဒေသမှ ကျောင်းသားများနှင့် နိုင်ငံခြားသားကျောင်းသားများကို ဘာသာစကားပညာရှင်များအဖြစ် လေ့ကျင့်ပေးနေပါသည်။

Mandalay University of Foreign Languages (MUFL) was established in 1997 as Upper Myanmar's premier and only specialized university for foreign language education. Located on 62nd Street (between 22nd and 23rd Streets) in Aungmyethazan Township, Mandalay, MUFL serves as the linguistic gateway for Upper Myanmar, training translators, interpreters, language teachers, and international business professionals.

As one of only two language universities in Myanmar (alongside Yangon University of Foreign Languages), MUFL offers full-time 4-year bachelor's degrees (BA) in 9 languages, 2-year Master of Arts (MA) programs, 1-year MA (Qualifying), and part-time diploma programs (Diploma in English, Postgraduate Diploma in English) run by the Centre of Human Resource Development. With over 1,600 students from Upper Myanmar and foreign countries, and 150+ academic staff, MUFL has produced thousands of graduates serving in government, international organizations, multinational corporations, and educational institutions across Upper Myanmar and beyond.

1997
Mandalay University of Foreign Languages established — Upper Myanmar's first and only specialized language university, serving Mandalay, Sagaing, and Shan States.
Late 1990s
Launched bachelor's degree programs in English, Chinese, Japanese, French, Korean, German, Russian, and Thai — becoming Upper Myanmar's language education hub.
2000s
Introduced Master of Arts programs in major languages and Burmese for international students — expanding postgraduate and international offerings.
2010s
Established Centre of Human Resource Development offering part-time Diploma in English and Postgraduate Diploma in English — serving working professionals in Upper Myanmar.
2024
Strengthened international partnerships including collaboration with Altai State Pedagogical University (Russia) for Russian Language and Culture Courses.
2020s
