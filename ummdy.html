About the University

ဆေးတက္ကသိုလ် (မန္တလေး) သည် မြန်မာနိုင်ငံ၏ ဒုတိယအသက်အကြီးဆုံးသော ဆေးပညာသင်ကြားရေးဌာနဖြစ်ပြီး ၁၉၅၄ ခုနှစ် ဧပြီလ ၁ ရက်နေ့တွင် ရန်ကုန်တက္ကသိုလ်၏ Branch Medical Faculty အဖြစ် မန္တလေးတက္ကသိုလ်ကျောင်းဝင်းတွင် တည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပါသည်။

University of Medicine, Mandalay (UMMdy) is Myanmar's second-oldest medical university, established on 1 April 1954. After relocating to its current site in Chanayethazan in 1955 and becoming an independent Institute of Medicine in 1964 — graduating its first M.B.,B.S. class of just 36 students — it grew into one of the country's most prestigious medical institutions. In 2005 it was renamed University of Medicine, Mandalay.

Today UMMdy is recognized by ECFMG and serves Upper Myanmar's primary medical education needs, training ~300–350 of the country's most competitive students each year across 13 affiliated teaching hospitals. Notably, students enter directly into the M.B.,B.S. program — no foundation year required — making it one of the most direct pathways to a medical degree in Myanmar.

Historical Milestones

1954
Founded as Branch Medical Faculty of University of Rangoon on 1 April, located on Mandalay University campus. First departments: Anatomy and Physiology.
1955
Relocated to its present site in Chanayethazan Township, Mandalay.
1956–57
Rapid expansion — Pharmacology, Pathology, Bacteriology, Medicine, Surgery, OB/GYN, Forensic Medicine, ENT, and Ophthalmology departments established.
1958
Became Faculty of Medicine, affiliated to University of Mandalay.
1964
Became independent Institute of Medicine, Mandalay. First M.B.,B.S. graduating class of 36 students. Foundation year departments (Child Health, Burmese, English, Sciences) established.
1968
Postgraduate programs began — first Master's degree in Physiology.
1991
Current main building complex completed.
2005
Officially renamed University of Medicine, Mandalay — its current name.
