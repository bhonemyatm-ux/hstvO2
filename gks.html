Scholarship Overview

The Global Korea Scholarship (GKS), formerly known as Korean Government Scholarship Program (KGSP), is one of Asia's most prestigious and comprehensive scholarship programs funded by the Korean government. The program offers full financial support for international students from over 160 countries to pursue undergraduate, Master's, and doctoral degrees at top Korean universities completely free.

GKS provides complete financial coverage including full tuition waiver (up to ₩5,000,000 per semester), generous monthly living stipend (₩1,000,000-₩1,500,000), round-trip airfare, settlement allowance, medical insurance, one-year Korean language training, research support, and completion grant. Myanmar students can apply through two tracks: Embassy Track (via Korean Embassy in Yangon) or University Track (directly to Korean universities).

✨

What Makes GKS Special?

GKS is funded directly by the Korean Government through the National Institute for International Education (NIIED) under the Ministry of Education. Unlike many scholarships, GKS includes mandatory 1-year Korean language training before degree programs, provides settlement allowance upon arrival, offers research support for graduate students, and gives completion grants. Scholars join a global network of 7,000+ GKS alumni worldwide with strong career prospects in Korea and internationally.

How to Apply

1

Choose Your Track: Embassy vs University

Embassy Track: Apply through Korean Embassy in Yangon, select 3 university preferences, more flexible. University Track: Apply directly to ONE specific university, requires university-specific motivation letter. Choose based on your strategy!

Visit Official Website →
2

Prepare All Required Documents (Start 2-3 Months Early)

Gather transcripts, diplomas, recommendation letters, personal statement, and proof of citizenship. Get all documents apostilled/notarized and translated to English or Korean. This takes time - don't underestimate it!

3

Download and Complete Application Forms

Download official GKS application forms (Form 1-6) from studyinkorea.go.kr. Fill out carefully in English or Korean. Double-check all information - errors can lead to disqualification!

Download Forms →
4

Write Compelling Personal Statement & Study Plan

Write a 2-page personal statement explaining your motivation, goals, why Korea, and why this program. For graduate students, prepare detailed study/research plan. Be specific, genuine, and realistic!

5

Submit Application Package

Embassy Track: Submit to Korean Embassy in Yangon by September 30, 2025. University Track: Submit directly to chosen university by October 31, 2025. Keep copies of everything!

6

Wait for First Round Results

Embassy Track results: October 17, 2025. University Track results: November 14, 2025. If you pass, proceed to NIIED review. If selected for embassy track, prepare consular-confirmed documents for second round.

7

Final Selection & University Placement

NIIED conducts final review (November-December). Universities make final admission decisions. Final results announced January 9, 2026. Successful candidates prepare for departure to Korea in August-September 2026!

📌 Important Application Notes:

  • Completely FREE: GKS application is 100% free. Never pay anyone for application help or "guaranteed admission"
  • Embassy Track vs University Track: Embassy Track has 3-step process (Embassy → NIIED → University). University Track has 2-step (University → NIIED)
  • Consular Confirmation Required: For embassy track second-round candidates, documents must be consularly confirmed
  • Expected Graduates: Can apply with certificate of expected graduation, but MUST submit official graduation certificate by December 31, 2025
  • Check Spam Folder: All notifications sent via email - check regularly!
04

Pro Tips for Success

🎯

Choose Your Track Strategically

Embassy Track: Better if you want flexibility (3 university choices) or unsure which university. University Track: Better if you have specific university/professor in mind. Consider Myanmar quota for each track before deciding!

📊

GPA Matters - Aim for Excellence

Minimum requirement is 80%, but successful applicants typically have 85-90%+ GPA. If your GPA is borderline, compensate with strong personal statement, awards, extracurriculars, or leadership experience.

✍️

Personal Statement is CRITICAL

Your 2-page personal statement can make or break your application. Be genuine, specific about why Korea and this program, explain how it connects to your goals. Avoid generic statements - show you've researched Korean education and culture!

📝

Get Documents Apostilled Early

Apostille/notarization process in Myanmar can take 2-4 weeks. Start early! All academic documents need apostille or notarization plus English/Korean translation. Don't leave this to last minute!

🎓

Strong Recommendation Letters

Choose recommenders who know you well academically or professionally. Letters should be specific about your strengths, achievements, and potential. Generic letters hurt your chances. Provide your recommenders with your CV and goals!

🏆

Highlight Achievements & Leadership

Include all academic awards, competitions, volunteer work, leadership positions, and extracurricular activities. GKS values well-rounded students. Create a compelling CV that showcases your unique strengths!

🇰🇷

Show Genuine Interest in Korea

Demonstrate knowledge of Korean culture, education system, and why studying in Korea specifically is important for your goals. Avoid applying just because it's free - show genuine interest and long-term vision!

👥

Connect with GKS Alumni

Contact current GKS scholars from Myanmar or join GKS alumni groups on Facebook. They provide invaluable insights about application process, university life, and interview tips. Learn from their experiences!
