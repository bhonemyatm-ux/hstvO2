About the University

ရေဇင် စိုက်ပျိုးရေး တက္ကသိုလ် သည် မြန်မာနိုင်ငံ၏ တစ်ခုတည်းသော စိုက်ပျိုးရေးတက္ကသိုလ် ဖြစ်ပါသည်။ ၁၉၂၄ ခုနှစ်တွင် စတင်တည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပြီး ၁၉၉၈ ခုနှစ်တွင် Yezin Agricultural University အဖြစ် အဆင့်မြှင့်တင်ခဲ့ပါသည်။

Yezin Agricultural University (YAU) is Myanmar's only agricultural university and the nation's premier institution for agricultural science education and research. Founded in 1924 as an agricultural college, it was transferred from the Ministry of Education to the Ministry of Agriculture and Irrigation in 1993 for better facilities and resources. In 1998, it was officially renamed Yezin Agricultural University.

Located in Zeyar Thiri Township, Nay Pyi Taw, YAU comprises 13 major academic departments, 8 academic supporting departments, and 2 research and knowledge centers. The university offers a five-year Bachelor of Agricultural Science (BAgrSc) degree with 10 specialized majors, a three-year Master of Agricultural Science (MAgrSc) program, and doctoral (PhD) programs in agricultural sciences. With 200+ academic staff and 2,500+ students, YAU has produced 15,000+ graduates serving as agricultural scientists, researchers, extension officers, agribusiness leaders, and policy makers across Myanmar and internationally. The university's vision is "To be a prime mover of agricultural and rural development in Myanmar through human resource development and national supplier of scientific knowledge and technology innovation."

Historical Milestones

1924
Agricultural College established during British colonial period — Myanmar's first institution dedicated to agricultural science education.
1993
Management transferred from Ministry of Education to Ministry of Agriculture and Irrigation — enhanced facilities, resources, and industry alignment.
1998
Renamed Yezin Agricultural University (YAU) — upgraded to university status with expanded programs and research capacity.
2001
Opened PhD program — becoming Myanmar's first institution to offer doctoral degrees in agricultural sciences.
2000s-2020s
Established 7 branch campuses across Myanmar (Aungban, Hlegu, Hmawbi, Kyaukse, Magway, Mawlamyine, Phyu) — expanding agricultural education access nationwide.
2020s
Modernized curriculum to include climate-smart agriculture, precision farming, agri-tech, and sustainable food systems — preparing graduates for 21st-century agricultural challenges.
