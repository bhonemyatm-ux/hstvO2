About the University

သွားဘက်ဆိုင်ရာ ဆေး တက္ကသိုလ် (ရန်ကုန်) သည် မြန်မာနိုင်ငံ၏ ပထမဆုံးနှင့် အထင်ရှားဆုံးသော သွားဘက်ဆိုင်ရာပညာသင်ကြားရေးဗဟိုဌာန ဖြစ်ပါသည်။ ၁၉၆၄ ခုနှစ်တွင် တည်ထောင်ခဲ့ပြီး ၂၀၀၃ ခုနှစ်တွင် တက္ကသိုလ်အဆင့်သို့ မြှင့်တင်ခဲ့ပါသည်။

University of Dental Medicine, Yangon (UDMY) traces its prestigious origins to 1964 when the Revolutionary Council established the College of Dental Medicine as Myanmar's only dental education institute, with technical cooperation from the British Government under the Colombo Plan. The first principal was Prof. Dr. U Aung Than, Oral and Plastic Surgeon of Yangon General Hospital, reinforced by Sir Robert Bradlaw from the United Kingdom.

Originally located at Yangon General Hospital campus (corner of Shwedagon Pagoda Road and Bogyoke Aung San Road), the institution was upgraded to Institute of Dental Medicine (IDM) in 1974. In 1997, IDM moved to its current purpose-built campus on Than Thu Mar Road, Thingangyun Township. Upgraded to university status in 2003, UDMY now offers Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS), Master of Dental Science (MDSc), Doctor of Dental Science (DrDSc), and PhD programs. With 1,058 students and 154 teaching staff, UDMY remains Myanmar's leading dental school, producing the nation's elite dentists and dental specialists.

Historical Milestones

1964
College of Dental Medicine established on 2nd November with 17 pioneer students — Myanmar's first dental school, affiliated to Institute of Medicine (1), Yangon.
1965
Student intake increased to 60 students annually — expanding dental education capacity.
1974
Upgraded to Institute of Dental Medicine (IDM) — teaching staff sent abroad for advanced training.
1993
Master of Dental Science (MDSc) program launched — 15-20 postgraduate candidates annually.
1997
Relocated to current Thingangyun campus on Than Thu Mar Road — modern facilities and expanded capacity.
2000
Undergraduate intake increased to 150 students, then 200 in 2002.
2002
Doctor of Dental Science (DrDSc) course commenced — 8 clinical disciplines for capacity building.
2003
Upgraded to University of Dental Medicine, Yangon — highest dental education status in Myanmar.
2005
50-Bed Teaching Hospital of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery inaugurated on campus — major clinical training milestone.
2011
PhD in Oral Biological Science program opened — advancing dental research in Myanmar.
2016
Dental Nurse Training Program reinstituted — supporting dental auxiliary workforce.
