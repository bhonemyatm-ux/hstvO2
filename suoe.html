About the University

Sagaing University of Education (SUOE) is one of Myanmar's two senior universities of education, dedicated to producing qualified, pedagogically grounded teachers for Myanmar's primary and secondary school system. The university was formally opened on 27 June 2000 as Sagaing Institute of Education (SIE), and was elevated to university status on 27 June 2014.

Located on a sprawling 152.38-acre campus in Sagaing — Myanmar's spiritual heartland — SUOE combines the academic rigor of a research university with the warmth of a teacher-training community. Under the leadership of Rector Dr. Myat Myat Thaw, the university maintains a student capacity of approximately 3,500.

SUOE's mandate extends beyond classroom instruction — its senior lecturers regularly deliver short-term pedagogical and professional development trainings to in-service teachers and educators across Myanmar, making it a national center of education expertise.

History Timeline

Early

Origins as Mandalay Institute of Education

The institution traces its roots to the Mandalay Institute of Education, serving as a regional hub for teacher training in upper Myanmar.

2000

Sagaing Institute of Education (SIE) Founded

Formally opened on 27 June 2000 in Sagaing as the Sagaing Institute of Education — a dedicated teacher education campus serving Sagaing Region and beyond.

2014

Upgraded to Sagaing University of Education (SUOE)

Elevated to full university status on 27 June 2014, enabling the university to offer doctoral-level research and expand its academic programs significantly.

Today

National Center for Teacher Education

SUOE operates as a public, non-profit university under Myanmar's Ministry of Education, recognized as one of the country's senior, regionally well-established universities of education.

Career Paths

SUOE graduates go on to lead Myanmar's education system at every level

👩‍🏫 Core Teaching Careers

The most common paths for SUOE graduates — teaching in government, private, and international schools across Myanmar.

👩‍🏫

Primary School Teacher

Teach foundational subjects (Myanmar, English, Maths, Science) in government, private, or international primary schools. SUOE B.Ed graduates are among the most sought‑after primary educators in Myanmar.

Career Progression:

Junior Teacher → Subject Head → Academic Coordinator → Deputy Head / Principal

250,000 – 600,000 MMK/month

Secondary School Teacher

Teach specialized subjects at middle and high school level in government and private schools. SUOE's curriculum gives graduates a strong subject‑specialist foundation for secondary education.

Junior Teacher → Subject Head → Academic Coordinator → Deputy Head / Principal

300,000 – 800,000 MMK/month
