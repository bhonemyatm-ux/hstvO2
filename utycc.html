About the University

The University of Technology, Yadanarpon Cyber City (UTYCC) is a public engineering and technology university founded in 2010, located within Yatanarpon Cyber City — a purpose-built technology park situated 28 miles along the Mandalay–Lashio road in Pyin Oo Lwin Township, Mandalay Region. In Burmese, the university is known as နည်းပညာတက္ကသိုလ် (ရတနာပုံ ဆိုင်ဘာ စီးတီး).

UTYCC stands apart from Myanmar's other engineering universities through its unique specialization in cutting-edge technology disciplines — including Information Science and Technology, Computer Engineering, Electronics Engineering, Mechanical Precision and Automation, and Advanced Materials and Metallurgy Engineering. These are fields not widely offered at other national engineering universities, giving UTYCC graduates a distinct competitive edge in technology and research sectors.

The university is home to an active research arm at utycc-research.org, with particular strengths in cybersecurity (including lattice-based cryptographic systems such as FALCON), artificial intelligence, sustainable technology, and e-learning innovation. UTYCC aspires to become a Southeast Asia research hub contributing to Myanmar's digital transformation through industry and international partnerships.

Since September 1, 2021, UTYCC has been managed by an Interim Council comprising teacher representatives, student union representatives, and alumni — operating under the National Unity Government's Ministry of Education policies and committed to the continuity of learning for its students.

History Timeline

2010

UTYCC Founded

University of Technology, Yadanarpon Cyber City was established within Myanmar's Yatanarpon Cyber City technology park — a planned digital district near Pyin Oo Lwin in Mandalay Region, designed to anchor Myanmar's ICT development.

2010s

Rapid Institutional Growth

UTYCC experienced rapid growth across its faculties — establishing 4 specialized faculties covering ICT, Electronics Engineering, Advanced Materials Engineering, and Precision Engineering alongside its 5 core B.E. programs and growing postgraduate offerings.

2021

Interim Council Established (Sept 1, 2021)

An Interim Council comprising teacher, student, and alumni representatives assumed governance of UTYCC, continuing the university's academic programs and research activities under the National Unity Government's Ministry of Education framework.

Campus Life at UTYCC

Life at UTYCC is shaped by its extraordinary setting inside Yatanarpon Cyber City — a purpose-built ICT and technology park located 28 miles along the Mandalay–Lashio road in the cool highlands of Pyin Oo Lwin, Mandalay Region. Students experience a uniquely modern, technology-focused campus environment that reflects the future UTYCC is building towards.

🌐

Yatanarpon Cyber City Environment

Living and studying inside Myanmar's Cyber City technology park means students are immersed daily in a purpose-built digital infrastructure district — with modern road networks, reliable utilities, and a technology-industry ecosystem surrounding the campus.

🏔️

Pyin Oo Lwin Highland Setting

Located near Pyin Oo Lwin (formerly known as Maymyo) — famous for its cool climate, colonial-era gardens, and scenic highlands — UTYCC students enjoy a refreshing natural environment at altitude alongside their technology-intensive studies.

🔬

Specialized Engineering Laboratories

Dedicated labs for each of UTYCC's four faculties — including computer labs, electronics workshops, materials characterization facilities, and precision engineering workspaces — enabling hands-on learning aligned with each program's cutting-edge focus.

🖥️

ICT & Research Facilities

State-of-the-art computer and networking facilities support UTYCC's ICT programs and research activities — including cybersecurity research, AI development, and e-learning infrastructure that has achieved 80% student adoption.

🏠

Student Hostels

On-campus dormitories accommodate students from across Myanmar — creating a close-knit residential academic community within the Cyber City grounds and enabling full immersion in the university's technology-focused culture.

🤝

Student Union & Alumni Engagement

UTYCC has an active student union and engaged alumni community — with regular online and in-person engagement sessions, alumni meetings, and collaborative initiatives that connect current students with graduates across Myanmar and internationally.

Alumni & Community

Since its founding in 2010, UTYCC has produced a growing cohort of alumni who are distinguished by their specialization in fields that remain rare in Myanmar's engineering workforce — cybersecurity, AI, precision engineering, advanced materials, and computer engineering. UTYCC graduates carry a distinctive profile in the technology and engineering job market.

💻

Technology Sector Leaders

IST and CE graduates from UTYCC are in strong demand across Myanmar's technology sector — from software companies and telecom operators to government IT departments and digital infrastructure projects — bringing specialized computing and software expertise that few other universities provide.

🔐

Cybersecurity & Research

UTYCC's research orientation means alumni frequently progress into research roles, postgraduate study, and cybersecurity-focused positions — areas of rapidly growing importance as Myanmar's digital economy expands and security demands increase.

🌏

International Opportunities

UTYCC's specialized programs in Computer Engineering, Precision Engineering, and Advanced Materials align with international industry demands — giving graduates pathways to postgraduate study and careers in Japan, South Korea, Singapore, and other technology-advanced nations.

🎓

Active Alumni Network

UTYCC's alumni association remains actively engaged with the university — participating in student mentoring, fundraising initiatives, Zoom-based meeting programs, and collaborative efforts to support the continued academic community through recent challenges.
