About the University

Yangon University of Education (YUOE) stands as Myanmar's premier and most prestigious university of education, producing the country's most influential classroom teachers, school leaders, education researchers, and academic lecturers. Located at No. 317, Pyay Road, Kamayut Township, Yangon, it sits at the heart of Myanmar's most important city and its education system.

With an estimated enrollment of 25,000 to 29,999 students, YUOE is one of Myanmar's largest universities by size — reflecting the enormous national demand for qualified teachers. As a public, non-profit institution under the Ministry of Education, the university has shaped generations of educators who went on to teach in every corner of Myanmar.

YUOE's academic library traces its heritage to the Faculty of Education library at Rangoon University in 1946, now evolved into a modern, purpose-built academic library serving tens of thousands of education students and researchers.

History Timeline

1931

Teacher Training College (TTC) Established

The predecessor institution, the Teacher Training College (TTC), was founded in 1931 — making YUOE one of Myanmar's oldest teacher education institutions, with over 90 years of continuous history.

1946

Faculty of Education, Rangoon University

The institution was integrated into Rangoon (Yangon) University as the Faculty of Education. The Faculty of Education library, established in this period, became the academic heritage of today's YUOE library.

Modern

Yangon University of Education (YUOE)

Now operates as an independent Yangon University of Education, counted among Myanmar's 161 tertiary-level institutions. Recognized internationally by databases such as uniRank as one of Myanmar's large-sized public universities.

Today

Myanmar's Premier Education University

YUOE is recognized as the premier university of education in Myanmar, leading the country in teacher preparation, education research, and national pedagogy standards.

Research & Scholarship

YUOE leads Myanmar's education research and policy development

✅ Key Research Areas at YUOE

  • National curriculum development and design for Myanmar's primary and secondary education system
  • Teacher education reform, pre-service and in-service training models
  • Educational assessment, evaluation, and examination standards
  • Educational psychology — motivation, learning styles, and student development
  • Inclusive and special needs education research
  • EdTech integration and blended learning in Myanmar classrooms
  • Education policy analysis in collaboration with Myanmar's Ministry of Education
  • Language teaching pedagogy — Myanmar language and English in education
