Overview

The Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship is one of the world's most comprehensive funded scholarship programmes — awarding roughly 9,000 scholarships to international students each year for undergraduate, master's, and PhD studies at top Japanese universities.

MEXT covers a full tuition waiver, a generous monthly stipend (¥117,000–¥145,000), round-trip airfare, and intensive Japanese language training. For Myanmar applicants, the process is run by the Myanmar Association of Japan Alumni (MAJA) in coordination with the Embassy of Japan in Yangon.

✦

What sets MEXT apart

MEXT is funded directly by Japan's Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology. It offers one of the longest scholarship durations available — up to seven years for graduate students — includes mandatory Japanese language training, and guarantees university placement. Scholars join an alumni network spanning more than sixty years and over a hundred countries.

May 7 – 14 (Research) · May 20 – 26 (UG) →

4

Written examination

Sit the written test at the designated exam centre in Yangon — language proficiency and subject-specific topics in your field.

May 17 (Research) · May 30 – 31 (UG) →

5

Interview

Qualified candidates are invited to interview at the Embassy of Japan in Yangon. Be ready to discuss your study plan, motivation, and post-graduation goals in detail.

May 31 – June 1 (Research) · June 7 – 8 (UG) →

6

University placement

If selected, MEXT will coordinate placement based on your preferences. You may need to request a Letter of Acceptance from your preferred professor or university.

Final notification & departure

Successful candidates receive an official notification by email. Begin the visa application and prepare to depart for Japan in April 2027.

Important notes

  • The process is free. Never pay anyone for MEXT application assistance.
  • Use only official channels. For Myanmar, that means MAJA.
  • Keep copies of every submitted document and email.
  • Check your email regularly after submission for updates.
04

Tips for a successful application

🎯

Start early

Begin preparation at least a year before applying. MEXT receives thousands of applications worldwide — strong preparation is what makes you stand out.

📊

Perfect your research plan

For graduate applicants, this is the single most important document. Be specific, realistic, and demonstrate clear command of your field. Vague or overly ambitious plans are rejected.

🎓

Contact professors early

Research potential supervisors at Japanese universities six or more months before applying. A professor's preliminary acceptance dramatically strengthens your application.

📝

Documentation has to be flawless

Every document must be properly certified, translated to English, and clearly organised. Missing or incorrect documents mean automatic rejection.

💪

Aim well above the minimum GPA

The minimum is 2.30 / 3.00, but successful candidates typically have 2.80+. Strong grades are a major signal.

🗣️

Practise the interview

Be able to explain your study plan clearly and confidently in English. Prepare for questions on motivation, research methodology, and post-graduation plans.

🌸

Show genuine interest in Japan

Demonstrate why Japan in particular matters for your goals — its culture, language, research community, or industry.

👥

Connect with alumni

Reach out to MAJA and current MEXT scholars from Myanmar. Their insights on the application and life in Japan are invaluable.

05

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to know Japanese to apply? +

No. Japanese proficiency is not required at application. MEXT provides 6 – 12 months of intensive Japanese training before your degree begins — one of the unique benefits of the programme.

Is ¥117,000 – ¥145,000 per month enough to live in Japan? +

Yes. The stipend is calculated to cover living costs — accommodation, food, transport, and personal expenses. Many MEXT scholars manage to save. Tuition is fully covered separately.

Can I choose which university to attend? +

You may list up to three preferred universities. MEXT coordinates placement based on your preferences, academic background, and professor availability — final placement is at MEXT's discretion.

How competitive is MEXT for Myanmar students? +

Highly competitive. Roughly 200 – 300 Myanmar students apply each year, with about 10 – 15 selected — a success rate near 3 – 5%. A strong academic record, excellent research plan, and confident interview are essential.

Can I work part-time on a MEXT scholarship? +

Yes — with permission from your university and immigration. Up to 28 hours per week during semester and full-time during breaks. MEXT scholars are expected to prioritise their studies.

What happens after I complete my degree? +

You're expected to return to Myanmar — MEXT is designed to develop future leaders who contribute to their home countries. You can return to Japan later for work or further studies through other programmes.
