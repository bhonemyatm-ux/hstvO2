Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship 2026

Full Funding for Master's, PhD & Undergraduate Studies in Japan

Organization: Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science & Technology (MEXT)
Last Updated: January 2, 2026
⏰

Application Deadline 2026

Research Students: April 14 - May 14, 2026

Undergraduate: April 22 - May 26, 2026

Applications open mid-April through Myanmar Association of Japan Alumni (MAJA)

📋 Overview

The Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship is one of the most prestigious and comprehensive scholarship programs for international students, offering approximately 9,000 scholarships annually worldwide. The program provides full financial support for undergraduate and graduate studies at Japanese universities.

For Myanmar students, applications are processed through the Myanmar Association of Japan Alumni (MAJA) in coordination with the Embassy of Japan. The scholarship covers full tuition, monthly living allowance, airfare, and Japanese language training.

💰 Scholarship Coverage

✈️

Travel Expenses

Round-trip airfare between Myanmar and Japan (at start and end of scholarship)

🎓

Full Tuition

100% tuition fees, entrance exam fees, and matriculation fees covered

💵

Monthly Stipend

Research Students: ¥143,000/month
Master's Students: ¥144,000/month
PhD Students: ¥145,000/month
Undergraduate: ¥117,000/month

🏠

Regional Allowance

Additional ¥2,000-3,000/month for designated regions with higher cost of living

🗣️

Language Training

Free Japanese language preparatory course (6 months to 1 year) if required

🏥

Visa Support

Student visa sponsorship and university placement assistance

📚 Program Types & Duration

1. Research Student Scholarship (Graduate Level)

Duration: Up to 7 years (including preparatory course)

  • 1.5-2 years: Research course (preparatory, non-degree)
  • 2 years: Master's degree program
  • 3 years: PhD program (optional)

Eligibility: Bachelor's degree holders (including prospective graduates), under 35 years old

2. Undergraduate Student Scholarship

Duration: 5 years total

  • 1 year: Preparatory course (Japanese language + EJU exam preparation)
  • 4 years: Bachelor's degree program

Eligibility: High school graduates or prospective graduates, age 17-22 years old

3. Teacher Training Scholarship

Duration: 1.5 years

Eligibility: University graduates with at least 5 years teaching experience, under 35 years old

✅ Eligibility Requirements

General Requirements:

  • Must be a Myanmar national currently living in Myanmar
  • Must have graduated from a Myanmar or foreign university
  • Must maintain minimum GPA of 2.30/3.00 (or equivalent)
  • Must be in good health, both physically and mentally
  • Must not be already enrolled in a Japanese university

Age Limits (as of April 1, 2026):

  • Undergraduate: Born between April 2, 2001 and April 1, 2007 (17-22 years)
  • Research/Graduate: Born on or after April 2, 1991 (under 35 years)
  • Teacher Training: Born on or after April 2, 1991 (under 35 years)

Academic Requirements:

  • Undergraduate: Completed 12 years of education (high school diploma)
  • Master's: Bachelor's degree (16 years of education)
  • PhD: Master's degree (18 years of education)

Language Requirements:

  • Japanese language proficiency is NOT required at application
  • English proficiency certificate (TOEFL/IELTS) is recommended but not mandatory
  • If your university taught in English, submit an English Proficiency Certificate from your institution

📝 Application Process for Myanmar Students

1

Registration (April 14 - May 6, 2026)

Register through Google Form provided by MAJA. Detailed information available at studyinjapan.go.jp

2

Document Submission (May 7-14, 2026)

Submit complete application documents to MAJA office in Thingangyun Township, Yangon (in person or by post) by 3:00 PM on May 14

3

Written Test (May 17, 2026)

Eligible candidates announced May 15. Written examination in Japanese/English and subject-related topics held at Yangon exam station

4

Interview (May 31 - June 1, 2026)

Test results announced May 20. Qualified candidates invited for interview conducted by Embassy of Japan

5

University Placement (June - September 2026)

Successful candidates request Letter of Acceptance from Japanese universities. MEXT coordinates university placement

6

Final Results (Around June 2026)

MEXT announces final scholarship recipients. Successful candidates receive notification via email

7

Departure to Japan (April 2027)

Scholars depart for Japan to begin preparatory course or degree program

📄 Required Documents

☑
MEXT Application Form

Download from studyinjapan.go.jp or Embassy website, completed in English

☑
Academic Transcripts

Official transcripts from all universities attended, with grading system explanation, verified with official stamps. English translation required if original not in English

☑
Graduation Certificate/Diploma

Official certificate verified with stamps. Prospective graduates must submit expected graduation certificate. English translation required if not in English

☑
Research Plan / Study Plan

For graduate students: Detailed research proposal (1,000-1,500 words) explaining motivation, research background, and prospective study. Must be specific and realistic

☑
Recommendation Letters (2)

For graduate students: Letters from professors/advisors/employers who can evaluate academic performance and research potential, written in English

☑
Curriculum Vitae (CV)

Detailed CV including education, work experience, publications, awards, and relevant activities

☑
Passport Copy

Scanned copy of valid passport (if available at time of application)

☑
Photographs

Recent passport-sized photos (specifications provided in application guidelines)

☑
English Proficiency Certificate

TOEFL/IELTS scores OR certificate from your university confirming English as medium of instruction

☑
Academic Publications (if applicable)

For research students: Copies of academic papers, thesis, or relevant publications

☑
Google Form Registration Screenshot

Proof of online registration through MAJA's Google Form system

💡 Tips for Successful Application

🎯 Start Early

Begin preparation at least 1 year before the application deadline. MEXT is highly competitive with thousands of applicants worldwide

📊 Strong Research Plan

Many strong candidates are rejected due to weak research proposals. Be specific, realistic, and show clear understanding of your field. Research potential supervisors and universities

🎓 Contact Professors

Before applying, contact potential supervisors at Japanese universities. A professor's acceptance significantly strengthens your application

📝 Perfect Documentation

Ensure all documents are properly certified, translated (if needed), and clearly organized. Incomplete applications are automatically rejected

🗣️ Interview Preparation

Practice explaining your research/study plan clearly and confidently. Be prepared to discuss your motivation, future goals, and why Japan

📚 Academic Excellence

Maintain strong academic record. While minimum is 2.30/3.00 GPA, successful candidates typically have much higher grades
