Scholarship Overview

The Chinese Government Scholarship (CGS), administered by the China Scholarship Council (CSC), is one of the world's largest and most comprehensive international scholarship programmes. Established by the Ministry of Education of China, CSC provides fully-funded scholarships to tens of thousands of international students annually to study at over 300 prestigious Chinese universities. Myanmar students can apply through multiple pathways: Type A (via Chinese Embassy in Myanmar), Type B (direct to universities), or Type C (through bilateral exchange programmes).

CSC scholarships cover all academic levels - from undergraduate through PhD - in virtually every field of study including engineering, medicine, sciences, business, Chinese language and culture, arts, and more. The scholarship provides comprehensive support: full tuition waiver, free on-campus accommodation (dormitory), monthly living stipend (¥2,500-3,500 depending on degree level), comprehensive medical insurance, and one-time settlement allowance. With China's rapidly growing global influence, world-class research facilities, and affordable living costs, CSC represents an excellent opportunity for Myanmar students seeking quality international education in Asia's largest economy.

✨

Why Choose CSC Scholarship?

CSC is one of the MOST ACCESSIBLE major scholarships for Myanmar students! China offers THOUSANDS of slots (not limited quotas like other countries), making acceptance rates higher. You get EVERYTHING: full tuition + FREE dormitory accommodation + monthly stipend (¥2,500-3,500 = ~$350-490 USD) + medical insurance + settlement allowance. Study at top universities like Tsinghua, Peking, Fudan, Zhejiang - many ranked globally! Living costs are affordable (¥2,000-3,000/month), Chinese degrees are increasingly valued worldwide, and you can study in English OR Chinese (1-year free Chinese prep course included!). Multiple application routes increase chances - apply through embassy AND universities simultaneously!

Scholarship Details

📋

CSC Scholarship Types

Three Application Categories (Choose Strategically!):

  • Type A (Embassy Route): Apply through Chinese Embassy in Yangon - can apply to 2 universities. Embassy recommends strongest candidates to CSC.
  • Type B (University Route): Apply directly to Chinese university - can only choose 1 university per application. University nominates you to CSC.
  • Type C (Bilateral/Exchange): Through special bilateral agreements or exchange programs between Myanmar and China.
  • NEW RULE 2026: Each student can submit maximum 3 applications total (max 2 Type A + 1 Type B) per enrollment year!
  • Strategy: Apply Type A through embassy (2 unis) + Type B direct (1 uni) = 3 chances!

How to Apply

1

Research Universities & Programmes

Visit www.campuschina.org to browse 300+ CSC-affiliated universities. Research programmes, check if English-taught options available, review admission requirements. Popular choices: Tsinghua, Peking, Fudan, Shanghai Jiao Tong, Zhejiang, Nanjing. Consider city location, programme strength, language of instruction.

Browse Universities →
2

Get Documents Notarized & Translated

CRITICAL STEP! Get ALL educational documents (diplomas, transcripts) officially notarized and translated to English or Chinese. This takes 2-3 weeks minimum! Also download CSC Physical Examination Form from campuschina.org and get it completed by certified doctor within 6 months of application.

3

Contact Universities for Pre-Admission (Type B)

If applying Type B (direct to university), contact university international office or professors in your field. Some universities require pre-admission letter before scholarship application! Email prospective supervisors for Master's/PhD. Some universities have rolling admissions - apply early!

4

Complete Online Application

Register on www.campuschina.org (or www.csc.edu.cn/studyinchina). Fill online application form carefully. Upload all required documents as PDFs. Write compelling Study Plan (800-1000 words) explaining why China, why this university/programme, your goals, how you'll contribute to China-Myanmar relations.

5

Choose Application Type Strategically

**Type A (Embassy):** Submit application through Chinese Embassy in Yangon by Feb 23 - can select 2 universities. **Type B (University):** Apply directly to 1 university by their deadline (Dec-Apr). **Pro tip:** Do BOTH! Apply Type A first (2 unis), then Type B (1 uni) = 3 total chances!

6

Get Recommendation Letters

Request two strong recommendation letters from professors or employers who know you well. Letters should be on official letterhead, signed, and address your academic abilities, character, potential for success in China. Give recommenders at least 2-3 weeks notice!

7

Submit & Monitor Results

Submit complete application before deadlines. Print confirmation page. Type A: Embassy forwards to CSC by early March. Type B: University reviews and nominates you. Check application portal regularly for updates. Results announced July-August 2026. If accepted, download Admission Notice and JW201 visa form, then apply for X1/X2 student visa!

📌 Critical Success Tips:

  • Start in December: Portal opens Dec 15 - start immediately to have time for document preparation!
  • Apply to Multiple Routes: Max 3 applications (2 Type A + 1 Type B) - use all opportunities!
  • Notarization is Non-Negotiable: Spend time getting proper notarization and translation of all documents
  • Chinese Prep Course Option: Don't worry about not knowing Chinese - select prep course option!
  • Contact Embassy: Visit Chinese Embassy in Yangon for guidance on Type A application process
04

Pro Tips for Success

🎯

Apply Through BOTH Routes

Maximize chances! Apply Type A through embassy (2 universities, deadline Feb 23) AND Type B direct to 1 university (deadline varies). That's 3 applications total! Embassy route is often easier for Myanmar students. University route gives you more control over programme selection!

🗣️

Don't Fear the Language Barrier

No Chinese? NO PROBLEM! CSC provides FREE 1-year intensive Chinese prep course! Or choose English-taught programmes (available at many universities especially for Master's/PhD). Even without HSK certificate, you can apply and take prep year. This is huge advantage over other countries!

📝

Strong Study Plan is KEY

Your 800-1000 word study plan is critical! Be specific: Why this field? Why China specifically? Why this university? How does it relate to Myanmar's development? What will you do after graduation? Show genuine interest in China-Myanmar cooperation. Research professors in your field and mention them!

🏛️

Consider "Hidden Gems" Universities

Everyone applies to Tsinghua/Peking - competition is fierce! Consider excellent but less famous universities: Wuhan, Chongqing, Dalian, Xiamen, Nanjing University of Science & Technology. Still high quality, better acceptance rates, lower living costs, great Chinese cultural experience!

💰

Stipend is Actually Sufficient!

Living costs in China: ¥2,000-3,000/month (food, transport, entertainment). Your stipend (¥2,500-3,500/month) COVERS this! Plus free accommodation! You won't live luxuriously but you'll be comfortable. Budget ¥500-800 for initial airfare and settling-in expenses.

📄

Notarization Process Matters

Get documents notarized by authorized notary public, then translate by certified translator. Each page needs official stamp! If documents are in Myanmar language, translate to English first, then notarize. This costs money (¥50-200 per document) and takes time - budget both!

🏥

Physical Exam Form is Mandatory

Download official CSC Physical Examination Form (specific form required!). Get it completed by certified doctor/hospital. Must include blood tests, X-ray, full check-up. Valid for 6 months only - time it right! Bring doctor's official stamp/signature. Hospital charges vary (¥100-300).

🌏

Connect with Myanmar Students in China

Join Facebook groups: "Myanmar Students in China", "CSC Scholarship Myanmar", "Myanmar in China". Ask current students about: which universities are Myanmar-friendly, living experiences, application tips, which cities are better for Myanmar students. Their advice is invaluable!
